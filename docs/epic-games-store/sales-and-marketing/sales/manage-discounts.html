Manage Discounts

Create and manage discount campaigns for your product offers.

16 mins to read

This article covers functionality offered by the self-service Epic Games Store publishing tools. If the options described are not available to you, you may be using an alternate method of distribution. For documentation applicable to your experience, sign in to the Knowledge Base

Overview

In the Developer Portal (Dev Portal), you can create and manage discounts for your offers.

When you apply a discount to an offer, the storefront displays strikethrough pricing. This means that the storefront displays the discounted price alongside the regular price to highlight the comparison to users.

In the Epic Games Store publishing tools, discounts are defined by two primary elements:

  • Duration: The length of time the discount is available on the storefront, as determined by its start and end dates/times.
  • Discount value: The percentage decrease applied to the offer's base price, such as 25% off.

In the Dev Portal, you can create two types of discount for your offers:

  • Custom discount: A discount with a customized campaign name and start and end dates provided by your organization.
  • Preset campaign: A discount that is associated with an Epic Games Store sale or an event that includes preset start and end dates (for example, the Epic Games MEGA Sale).

For both types of discount, you can specify the percentage decrease. However, some Epic-led preset campaigns require the discount value to be in a specific range.

There are guidelines for discounts on the Epic Games Store. When you create a discount, it automatically undergoes validation. This ensures that your discount follows the guidelines before it appears on the storefront.

Discount Guidelines

The Epic Games Store maintains a number of guidelines for discounts on the storefront. During creation, discounts undergo validation to verify that these policies have been followed.

The following discount guidelines apply on a per-product basis for both custom discounts and Epic Games preset campaigns.

  • You can apply only one discount to a product offer in a specific timeframe. In other words, multiple discounts cannot be active for a single offer at the same time.
  • You must submit the discount at least 24 hours before the discount start date and time.
  • The discount rate must be a value between 5% and 95%.
  • For post-launch discounts, the duration cannot exceed 30 consecutive days. For introductory offers, the following guidelines apply:
    • The duration cannot exceed seven consecutive days.
    • The discount amount must be a maximum of 40%.
    • Note: An introductory offer is a discount applied to your product offer at the time of its release. To learn more, see Create Introductory Offer on this page.
  • You cannot discount a product offer cannot for more than 45 days total during any 90-day window.
  • You must allow a minimum 30-day cooldown period between discounts that you apply to a specific product offer.
  • You cannot schedule discounts within the following time windows:
    • Within 30 days of a product’s launch date.
    • Within 30 days of the end of an introductory offer.
  • Products in a pre-purchase state can have discounts, but the discounts start together with pre-purchase and end when the product launches. You can convert the discount to an introductory offer after the product launches. However, the following guidelines apply:
    • The discount must be the same discount value as the introductory offer.
    • You must configure the discount at the same time as you configure the pre-purchase discount.
  • You cannot schedule a discount for a date within 30 days of a price change. In other words, you must schedule discounts for a date at least 31 days after a price change.

Create and Submit Discounts

Create Custom or Preset Campaign Discount

In the Dev Portal, you can create both custom and preset campaign discounts for your product offers.

  • Custom discounts allow you to create your own discount campaigns with customized start and end dates. These dates determine when a discount is active on the storefront.
  • Preset campaigns are associated with an Epic Games Store sale or event (for example, the Epic Games MEGA Sale) and include preset start and end dates. These dates cannot be modified.

To be eligible for a discount, a product offer must follow these guidelines:

  • It must be in the Live sandbox.
  • It must not be a demo offer.
  • It must not have a base price of $0.
  • It must have a specific launch date assigned. Discounts cannot be created for offers with an estimated or unknown launch date.
Discounts applied to offers at the time of their release must meet specific criteria. To learn more, see Create Introductory Offer on this page.

To create a custom or preset campaign discount, follow the steps below:

  1. In the Dev Portal, go to Your Product > Epic Games Store > Promo Campaigns > Discounts.

  2. In the top right, click Create Discount.

  3. In the Select Discount Type menu, choose Custom Discount or one of the Epic-led preset campaigns.

    • Note: The options are for upcoming campaigns only.

  4. In the Discount Info section, provide the appropriate details for your discount. Ensure the information adheres to our discount guidelines.

    • Custom discounts: Provide the discount name, start date/time, end date/time, and discount value.
      • Note: To easily identify discounts, we recommend you use a naming convention that clearly conveys the timeframe and content of the campaign. Example: [Publisher Sale] Epic Games - Spring ‘23 or [Franchise Sale] Rocket League - Summer ‘23.
    • Preset campaigns: Provide the discount value. All other details are provided by Epic Games and cannot be modified.

  5. In the Offers section, select each offer to include in the discount. You can then set the discount value individually per offer or apply the same discount to all selected offers.

    • Individual discounts: In the Discount % column, enter the desired decrease for each selected offer.
    • Bulk discount: In the Discount Info section, enter the desired decrease in the Bulk Discount % field. This value is automatically applied to all selected offers.
    • Note: The Offers section only contains offers in the Live sandbox, excluding any Demo-type offers or those with a base price of $0.

  6. Review the details of your discount to ensure accuracy.

  7. Click Submit.

    • Your discount automatically undergoes validation to ensure that it adheres to our guidelines. Then, if the discount review process is applicable for your organization, it is automatically sent to the Epic Games Store team for approval. See Submit Discounts for Validation and Review on this page for more information.
    • If the discount does not adhere to our discount guidelines, a validation error displays at the top of the screen. The Offers section shows error details for each affected offer. Update your discount as needed to resolve the issues, then click Submit again.

When the discount is created, it displays in the table on the Discounts page. Its status is as follows:

  • If the discount review process is not applicable for your organization, its status is Published.
  • If the discount review process is applicable for your organization, its status is Queued for review.

On the discount start date, the discount goes live on the storefront, and its status is updated to Active.

You can edit or delete a discount as needed before the discount's start date.

For more information, see Manage Discounts on this page.

Create Introductory Offer

An introductory offer is a discount applied to a product offer at the time of the product's release, such as a launch day sale. The product offer must revert to full price no later than seven days after release.

The discount created for an introductory offer must meet the following criteria:

  • The start date and time of the discount must exactly match the product offer’s launch date and time.
  • The discount rate must be between 5% and 40%.
  • The duration cannot be more than seven days.

The product offer receiving the discount must have a specific release date assigned that matches its effective date (for example, the date the product offer becomes purchasable on the storefront). Make a note of this date and time as you need this information to create your introductory offer.

To create an introductory offer, follow the steps below:

  1. In the Dev Portal, go to Your Product > Epic Games Store > Offers.

  2. Select the appropriate sandbox.

  3. Create a new offer or update an existing offer.

  4. Set the offer's Launch Date to the date and time of your product launch. Take note of this date and time because you need this information to create your discount.

  5. In the Dev Portal, go to Your Product > Epic Games Store > Release Management.

  6. Promote your offer to the Live sandbox. You must complete this step before you create your discount. Note: The offer will not be visible to users on the storefront until its Early Store Discovery or Launch Date occurs.

  7. In the Dev Portal, go to Your Product > Epic Games Store > Promo Campaigns > Discount Management Tool.

  8. Create a new custom discount as follows:

    • Set the Start Date and Time to the launch date and time of your offer.
    • Set the End Date and Time to no later than seven days after the start date and time.
    • Set the Discount Rate to a value between 5% and 40%.
    • In the Offers section, select the offer from step 3.

  9. Carefully review the details of your discount.

  10. Click Submit.

    • If any validation errors occur, you must resolve those issues before you can submit the discount again. To learn more, see discount guidelines on this page.
    • If the discount review process applies for your organization, then the introductory offer goes through the standard validation process. The offer is then automatically sent to the Epic Games Store team for review. To learn more, see Submit Discounts for Validation and Review below.

Submit Discounts for Validation and Review

When you submit your discount, it automatically goes through two processes, in the following order:

  • It goes through validation to ensure that it adheres to the Epic Games Store guidelines.
  • If the discount review process applies to your organization, it goes to the Epic Games Store team for review. The review process typically takes 24 hours (excluding weekends).

Discounts must pass validation and review before they can become active on the storefront.

You can monitor the progress of validation and approval on the Discount Management Tool page of the Dev Portal. The Status column indicates its current position in the workflow, as follows:

  • Queued for review: Your discount passed validation and is with the Epic Games Store team for review.
    • This only happens if the discount review process applies to the organization.
    • If the discount review process is not applicable for your organization, then status is updated to Published when your discount passes validation.
  • Validation failed: The discount did not pass validation. You must take additional action before the discount is sent for review. For more information, see Validation Failed below.

For more information on statuses, see Discount Statuses on this page.

Validation Failed

If your discount has the Validation failed status, follow the steps below to review the validation feedback:

  1. On the Discount Management Tool page, locate the discount with the Validation failed status.
  2. In the discount’s options (...) menu, select View.
  3. Review the provided feedback, then take one of the following actions:
    • Click Edit Discount and update your discount in accordance with the feedback. Then, resubmit the discount for validation again. and review.
    • If you cannot update your discount to adhere to our guidelines, select Submit with Issues Found to escalate your discount to the Epic Games Store team. This option is available only if the discount review process applies to your organization. You should only use this option after you have made reasonable efforts to resolve any issues.

After you submit the discount, its status updates as follows:

  • If the discount review process applies to your organization, it updates to Queued for review, and the discount is routed for review.
  • If the discount review process does not apply to your organization, it updates to Published, and the discount is published to use on the storefront.

After Review

After the Epic Games Store team reviews the discount, its status updates to one of the following:

  • Published: The discount is published and ready to become active on the storefront during its specified time frame.
  • Changes Needed: The review identified one or more issues with the discount. You must resolve these issues before you can publish the discount. Review the feedback comments provided by the Epic Games Store team and update the discount accordingly. Then, resubmit the discount for review again.

Upload Bulk Discounts

You can create discounts in bulk by filling out and uploading a provided .CSV template. With this template, you can create multiple discounts across multiple products with a single file upload.

Note: You cannot create custom and preset campaign discounts in a single upload. You must complete and upload a separate template for each of these discount types.

To upload bulk discounts, you must do the following:

  • Step 1. Download the template.
  • Step 2. Complete the template.
  • Step 3. Upload your completed template.

Step 1. Download the Template

To complete the bulk discount template, follow the steps below:

  1. In the Dev Portal, navigate to Your Product > Epic Games Store > Promo Campaigns > Discount Management Tool.
  2. Click Upload Bulk Discount.
  3. Select the type of discounts you want to create (for example, custom discounts or an available preset campaign).
  4. On the Upload Bulk Discount page, click Download .CSV Template.
    • Note: To ensure a successful file upload, do not change the template’s structure (for example, do not rename or add columns.)

Step 2. Complete the Template

Open the template. Some of the columns are pre-filled with your data as follows:

  • Product ID: A unique identifier for each product.
  • Offer ID: A unique identifier for each offer.
  • Offer name: (Optional). Use this field to label your offers so that you can identify them easily.
  • Current price: The current base price applied to the offer.

In the empty columns, enter the details as follows:

  • For all discount types, provide the following information:
    • Percentage: Integer value in the range of 1-100. Do not include the “%” symbol.
  • For custom discounts only, provide the following details:
    • Discount Start Date: Enter a date in the format YYYY-MM-DD.
    • Discount Start Time: Enter a time in the format HH:MM. Use a 24h format, UTC time zone.
    • Discount End Date: Enter a date in the YYYY-MM-DD date format.
    • Discount End Time: Enter a time in the format HH:MM. Use a 24h format, UTC time zone.
    • Discount Name: You can enter any text, except commas. The Discount Name must be unique in the product.

Step 3. Upload Your Completed Template

When you have completed the bulk discount template, upload the file in the Dev Portal. After confirming the information is correct, submit your discounts to the Epic Games Store.

  1. In the Dev Portal, go to Your Product > Epic Games Store > Promo Campaigns > Discount Management Tool.

  2. Click Upload Bulk Discount.

  3. Select the type of discounts you want to create (for example, custom discounts or an available preset campaign).

  4. In the Upload Bulk Discount panel, drag your completed .CSV into the box or click Browse and select the .CSV.

  5. Click Next.

  6. The Confirm Bulk Discount page displays an overview of your uploaded discounts. Review these discounts to ensure the information provided is accurate.

    • Note: To update any discounts before submission, complete the appropriate changes in the .CSV file. Then, click Clear & Re-Upload File in the Discount Request(s) section and upload the new template.

  7. Click Submit.

    • The submission process might take some time, depending on the number of discounts you create. Do not refresh or close the page until this process is complete.

After you click Submit, you are taken to the Discount Management Tool page while your discounts undergo validation. Validation helps expedite the approval process by ensuring discounts adhere to our guidelines. See Discount Guidelines on this page for details.

The table displays the discounts you created with bulk upload. Each discount has one of the following statuses:

  • Queued for review: Your discount was successfully sent for review. You do not need to take action at this time.

    • This status only applies if the discount review process applies for your organization.

  • Published: Your discount was successfully published on the storefront with the discount start date. You do not need to take action at this time.

  • Validation failed: The validation process found one or more issues with the discount. You must resolve these issues and resubmit the discount. See Submit Discounts for Validation and Review on this page for more information.

    • Note: You cannot resolve issues with the bulk upload process. You must update and resubmit each discount with issues separately. If you created discounts across multiple products with bulk upload, you must access the Discount Management Tool page for each product separately to review the feedback.

  • Error: An error occurred during the submission process. Access the View Discount page to review the error details.

Manage Discounts

On the Discount Management Tool page (Your Product > Epic Games Store > Promo Campaigns > Discount Management Tool), you can review your upcoming discounts, monitor their statuses, and edit and delete discounts.

Discount Statuses

The statuses given to discounts show their current position in the process. Possible statuses are as follows:

  • Draft: The discount has been created but not yet submitted. For example, you selected Save as Draft when you created the discount.

  • Validation failed: When you submitted the discount, the validation process found one or more issues. You can review specific issues on the View Discount page.

    • Note: See Discount Guidelines on this page for more information.

  • Queued for review: The discount is ready for review by the Epic Games Store team (if it applies for your organization). Submissions are typically reviewed within 24 hours (excluding weekends).

  • In review: The discount is under review by the Epic Games Store team (if it’s applicable for your organization).

  • Changes Needed: The Epic Games Store team identified one or more issues in the discount that must be resolved before it can be published. (if the discount review process is applicable for your organization). You can review specific issues on the View Discount page.

  • Published: The Epic Games Store team has published the discount for use on the storefront as scheduled. When the discount is live, its status is Active.

  • Active: The discount is currently live on the storefront.

  • Canceled: The discount was published but canceled before its start date. Canceled discounts do not become active on the storefront.

  • Expired: The end date of the discount has passed, and it is no longer active on the storefront.

  • Error: A failure occurred during the discount submission or cancellation process. You can review specific issues on the View Discount page.

  • Created by Epic: This discount was created by the Epic Games Store team. You cannot perform any actions on this discount.

    • If you want to make any changes, contact the Epic Games Store team. To do so, create a private discussion on our Developer Support site.

  • Created in SPT: This is a discount that you created by you in the previous version of the SPT Discounts interface. You cannot perform any actions on this discount.

Management Options

Several actions are available to help you manage your discounts. To take action on a discount, click the options (...) menu to the right of its row in the table. Then, select one of the options below:

  • View: Opens the View Discount page, which displays the following:
    • Specific details about the discount (such as its included offers).
    • Any validation issues or feedback from the Epic Games Store team (if the discount review process applies to your organization).
    • Note: On the View Discount page, to quickly switch to edit mode, click Edit Discount at the top of the page. You cannot edit discounts with the statuses In review, Active, Canceled, Expired, Error, Created by Epic, or Created in SPT.
  • Edit: Launches the discount in edit mode, where you can update its details. Discounts in some statuses cannot be edited.
    • You cannot edit discounts with the statuses In review, Active, Canceled, Expired, Error, Created by Epic, or Created in SPT.
    • If you update a discount, you submit it again.
  • Delete: Removes the discount from your product. You cannot delete a discount with the status published, canceled, or active.
  • Cancel: Prevents the discount from becoming active on the storefront on its start date.You can select this action only on discounts with the published status.
  • Clone: Use this action when you want to create a new discount based on this one. The Dev Portal copies the details from the discount into a new discount creation form. You can then provide additional information and submit the new discount.
    • Note: Start and end dates are not copied when you take this action.

Dashboard Calendar

Near the bottom of your product dashboard (Your Product > Epic Games Store), a discount calendar displays in the Promo Campaigns section. This calendar provides an overview of your upcoming discounts so you can quickly review your product’s discount schedule.
