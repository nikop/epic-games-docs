Publishing Tools Workflow

Recommended workflow for distributing products on the Epic Games Store

17 mins to read

This article covers functionality offered by the self-service Epic Games Store publishing tools. If the options described are not available to you, you may be using an alternate method of distribution. For documentation applicable to your experience, sign in to the Knowledge Base

Using the Epic Games Store Publishing Tools

The Epic Games Store publishing tools provide you with everything you need to distribute your product on the Epic Games Store. With these tools, you can maintain your product’s store presence, create offers, manage price tiers, and more.

To ensure a successful launch of your product on the storefront, follow our recommended publishing tools workflow. This workflow consists of five primary phases:

  • Onboarding
  • Publish Your Product Page (Early Awareness)
  • Finalize and Test Configurations
  • Submit for Final Review
  • Launch Your Product

Before getting started with the publishing tools, review the sections below to gain an understanding of the distribution flow. For each phase, technical steps and additional resources are provided to help you successfully launch your product on the Epic Games Store!

An estimated time to complete is provided for each phase to assist you with release planning efforts. Note that these timelines serve as general guidelines only and are subject to change. Timelines can vary due to a variety of factors, such as unique business needs, volume, or the level of configuration required.

Before You Begin

By default, new organizations can follow the standard onboarding process (outlined below) to access the Epic Games Store publishing tools.

  • If you already have an organization but have not yet implemented any Epic services for your product, the quickest way to access the publishing tools is to create a new organization and product in the Dev Portal.
  • If you have an organization with a product currently using Epic Online Services or Epic Account Services, reach out to the Epic Games Store team for assistance with enabling the publishing tools.

If you already have a product using Epic services, or otherwise wish to retain your current organization, request assistance by creating a private discussion on our Developer Support site. Set the Topic to Epic Games Store and include the following in your request:

  • Your organization ID
    • Tip: Available on the Settings tab of the Organization page
  • Your product ID(s)
    • Tip Available on the SDK Download & Credentials tab of the Product Settings page
  • One of the following options:
    • I have an existing organization, and I would like to create new products that can use the publishing tools.
    • I have an existing organization and product using Epic Online Services/Epic Accounts Services, and I am interested in using the publishing tools for this product.

Onboarding

Overview

To begin the distribution process, you first complete the Onboarding phase. This phase involves setting up your organization in the Dev Portal and completing the required onboarding tasks.

Estimated time to complete: Up to 10 business days, depending on your location.

Prerequisites: None

Before you begin: To complete this phase, prepare the following:

  • High-level details about your product or service, including its name and a short description
  • Payment information for the required submission fee
  • An individual who can complete the required agreements (i.e., the owner of your Dev Portal organization)
  • Tax information for your organization, such as W9/W8-BEN information
  • Payout account details for your organization (i.e., the account where Epic Games routes payments)

Process

  1. Access the Dev Portal and create an account if you do not already have one.
  2. Create your organization by clicking Create Organization and completing the brief form.
    • Update your organization type from Individual to Enterprise. (All new organizations are Individual by default.) This allows the owner of your organization to accept the required agreements on behalf of your organization as a legal signatory.
    • Complete additional setup for your organization as needed, such as inviting team members.
  3. Create your product by clicking Create Product and completing the brief form.
  4. Select your new product, then access the Epic Games Store section of the Dev Portal to launch the onboarding flow:
    • Accept the Epic Games Store agreements:
      • Required: Epic Games Store Distribution agreement
      • Recommended: Epic Games Store Email Sharing Rider
      • Recommended: Epic Achievements agreement
    • Pay the required per-product $100 USD submission fee.
      • Tip: If you have questions about the submission fee, create a private discussion on our Developer Support site for assistance.
    • Complete the preliminary product questionnaire. This questionnaire collects essential information about your product to help the Epic Games Store team understand how to best support you throughout the distribution process. Responses to the questionnaire are non-binding.
  5. Create your tax profile and activate your payout account.
    • Note: This process can take up to 2 weeks to complete.
  6. Review our Requirements & Guidelines to understand the policies for distributing products on the storefront.

Publish Your Product Page (Early Awareness)

Overview

Once the Onboarding phase is complete, you can publish your product page to the storefront.

  • If your product has not yet been released, complete this phase to spread early awareness of your product. We recommend publishing your product page when you announce your product to ensure users know it will be available on the Epic Games Store. This also allows users to wishlist your product and receive notifications (if they have opted-in).
  • If you have already released your product on other storefronts, you can optionally skip this step and proceed to Finalize and Test Configurations.

To publish your product page for early discovery, you must provide preliminary configurations for the following entities:

  • Store Settings
  • Offers
  • Pages
  • Localization (recommended)
You do not need to fully configure these entities at this point in the workflow. During the Finalize and Test Configurations phase, you can provide additional information and upload your product build to prepare for launch.

Estimated time to complete: 1 week

Prerequisites:

  • Complete the Onboarding phase.
  • Review Release Management to understand the sandbox environments in which entities are configured.

Before you begin: To complete this phase, prepare the following:

  • Metadata (e.g., genre and features), distribution regions, and pricing information for your product
  • Storefront content for your product page (text and media (e.g., images, videos))
  • Localized storefront content
    • Recommended: Localized text
    • Optional: Localized media

Process

  1. In the Dev Portal, select your product and access the Epic Games Store section.
  2. Configure your store settings:
    • Navigate to Store Settings > Product Configuration and complete the required sections with essential details about your product.
    • Navigate to Store Settings > Regions and Ratings to obtain an IARC rating (or upload traditional ratings) and select the regions in which you plan to distribute your product. While not required, getting an IARC rating for your product is recommended to best reach potential users.
  3. Configure your base product offer:
    • Access the Offers section and select your base product offer. (This offer is auto-generated when you first create your product and is assigned the Game type.)
    • Complete all required sections for your offer aside from Dates. A blue checkmark next to a section name indicates completion. Continue to Step 4 to configure the Dates section.
  4. Configure your dates of availability:
    • Navigate to Offers > Base Product Offer > Dates and complete the questionnaire to determine your store discovery and product launch dates.
      • Question 2 (When do you want this offer to be discovered?) determines when your product page is visible to users on the storefront. This date does not determine when it becomes available for purchase. You can set a specific availability date or choose to have your product page published as soon as your base product offer is pushed to the Live sandbox.
      • Question 3 (When is this offer officially launching, or anticipating to launch, on the Epic Games Store?) determines when your product is officially released on the storefront. If you do not have a product build ready, set the launch date type to Estimated or Unknown. If you set the launch date type to Specific, you are required to add a release-ready binary to the artifact for this offer.
        • If you select Unknown, the launch date on your product page displays as Coming Soon. If you select Estimated, you can provide a generalized date to display instead (e.g., May 2023).
        • If you select Estimated or Unknown, you must return during the Finalize and Test Configurations phase and provide a Specific launch date to prepare for release.
  5. Configure your product home page:
    • Navigate to Pages > Product Home and upload media assets for your product’s store presence.
      • Required:
        • Carousel: Upload videos and images to the featured media section that displays front and center on your product page.
        • About: Provide additional text that further describes your product or highlights certain features.
      • Optional:
        • Additional Images: Add images to a collapsible media grid on your product page.
        • Social Preview: Specify which image is used when sharing social media posts for your product.
  6. Localize your product page:
    • Access the Localization section and upload localized text and media for your product page. While not required, this step is recommended to best reach global users. The Epic Games Store currently supports 16 different locales.
      • Note: If you are unable to provide your own translations, the Epic Games Store offers a complimentary translation service that can localize your product page text free of charge.
  7. Once your configurations are complete, you can submit your product for review by the Epic Games Store team.
    • Access the Release Management section and click Push to Stage in the Dev sandbox. On the next screen, select the entities you configured in steps 2-6; these are required for publishing your product page. (If you have any additional offers or pages that are not ready to be published, leave those items unchecked.) Click Push to Stage. This action may take a few minutes to complete.
    • After the Push to Stage is complete, click Submit for Review in the Stage sandbox. The Epic Games Store team will then review your submission to ensure content guidelines and other requirements have been met. Review may take 2 or more days to complete depending on volume.
  8. Once your submission has been approved, publish your product page by clicking Push to Live in the Stage sandbox. The availability of your product page in the storefront depends on how you configured your discovery date in Step 4.
    • Note: When you push your product page to the Live sandbox, it becomes indexable on the public web, even if your discovery date is set to a future time. Ensure you are ready for your product to be public before completing this step.

Finalize and Test Configurations

Overview

Next, complete all of the configurations needed to launch your product, such as creating additional offers and uploading your build. Then conduct testing to ensure your product and store presence are ready for release. Once ready, you can then proceed with submitting your product for review by the Epic Games Store team.

Estimated time to complete: Varies per product depending on specific configurations needed.

Prerequisites:

  • Complete the Onboarding phase.
  • Review Release Management to understand the sandbox environments in which entities are configured.

Before you begin: To complete this phase, prepare the following:

  • A list of all offers (e.g., editions, season passes, etc.) you plan to release for your product, along with:
    • Metadata (e.g., genre and features), distribution regions, and pricing information for each offer
    • Storefront media and text for each offer
    • Localized content for each offer
      • Recommended: Localized text
      • Optional: Localized media
  • FAQ page content, if applicable
    • Tip: While FAQs are optional for most products, they may be required in certain situations. For example, FAQs are required for early access products or products featuring in-app purchases. To learn more, review the Epic Games Store distribution requirements.
  • The final binary or binaries for your product
    • Note: Ensure your binary meets the technical distribution requirements for the Epic Games Store and adheres to content guidelines. To learn more, review our Requirements & Guidelines.

Process

If you completed the Publish Your Product Page (Early Awareness) phase, you may have already taken some of these steps. In this case, remember to update your offer dates (especially the launch date) and upload binaries to prepare for launch.

  1. In the Dev Portal, select your product and access the Epic Games Store section.
  2. Configure your store settings:
    • Navigate to Store Settings > Product Configuration to provide high-level information about your product.
      • Tip: This section collects details and specifications for your product as a whole. If individual offer details differ from your overall product information, you can provide those details separately when configuring the offers.
    • Navigate to Store Settings > Regions and Ratings to obtain an IARC rating and select the regions in which you plan to distribute your product. While not required, getting an IARC rating for your product is recommended to best reach potential users.
  3. Configure your product offers:
    • Access the Offers section and select your base product offer. (This offer is auto-generated when you first create your product and is assigned the Game type.) Complete all sections of the Offer Details page as appropriate for your base product.
      • Note: If you already published your product page without attaching a binary to your base product offer, you must update its Launch Date Type to Specific and provide the date and time you plan to release your product. This is required in order to launch your product.
    • Create additional offers for your product as needed and provide all necessary offer details. You can create offers for editions, demos, add-ons, season passes, consumables, and digital extras.
  4. Configure your product home page:
    • Navigate to Pages > Product Home and provide text and media assets to populate your product’s store presence.
      • Required:
        • Carousel: Upload videos and images to the featured media section that displays front and center on your product page. To best reach users, including a video trailer for your product in the first position of the carousel is recommended.
        • About: Provide additional text that further describes your product or highlights certain features.
      • Optional:
        • Additional Images: Add images to a collapsible media grid on your product page.
        • Social Preview: Specify which image is used when sharing social media posts for your product.
  5. Configure pages for any additional offers. Pages are automatically generated for each offer associated with your product.
    • Access the Pages section and select the specific offer page you wish to edit. Then, provide text and media assets as needed to populate each offer page.
  6. Configure an FAQ page for your product, if applicable.
    • Access the Pages section, click Create Page, and select FAQ Page. Then, upload questions and answers about your product as needed.
    • FAQ pages are optional for most products; however, they are required for the following:
      • Products launched as early access titles
      • Products leveraging blockchain or NFT technology
      • Products containing in-app purchases
  7. Upload binaries and attach them to artifacts for your base product, editions, and other offers with downloadable content. This step must be completed before you can submit your product for review.
    • Navigate to the Artifacts and Binaries section and click Download Tool to download and install the BuildPatch tool. This tool helps you securely upload binary files for your product.
    • Use the BuildPatch tool to prepare your files, then upload your binaries and associate them with the appropriate artifacts through the Artifacts and Binaries page.
    • Test your binaries by downloading and installing them through the Epic Games Store launcher. In order for a tester to redeem an offer in private sandboxes, they must be a part of your organization and use a testing access key to access the artifact.
      • Tip: To ensure your build functions properly in the Epic Games Store launcher and meets the requirements for launch, we recommend following the steps in the Epic Games Store Testing Guide.
  8. Localize your store presence:
    • Access the Localization section and upload localized text and media for the pages that comprise your store presence. These pages include your product home page and store settings, as well as any additional offer and FAQ pages you configured for your product. While not required, this step is recommended to best reach global users. The Epic Games Store currently supports 16 different locales.
      • Note: If you are unable to provide your own translations, the Epic Games Store offers a complimentary translation service that can localize your product page text free of charge.
  9. Once your configurations are complete, you can push your product to the Stage sandbox.
    • Access the Release Management section and click Push to Stage in the Dev sandbox. On the next screen, select all entities you wish to include in your product launch. If you have additional offers or pages that are not yet ready for distribution, leave them unchecked. Click Push to Stage. This action may take a few minutes to complete.
      • Note: Some entities may have dependencies that prevent you from pushing them to Stage. You cannot push such entities until all dependencies are in the Ready for Stage status. During this step, ensure you select all dependencies for the entities included in your product launch.

Submit for Final Review

Overview

After finalizing and testing your product configurations, you can submit your product for review by the Epic Games Store team. During this phase, your content and builds are reviewed to ensure they meet our content guidelines and requirements for distribution.

Estimated time to complete: Varies depending on volume and product complexity.

Prerequisites:

  • Complete the Onboarding phase.
  • Complete the Finalize and Test Configurations phase.

Before you begin:

  • Review the Epic Games Store distribution requirements and content guidelines to ensure your product is ready for launch. During this phase, the Epic Games Store team will verify that your submission is complete and compliant with our policies.

Process

  1. Navigate to the Release Management section and click Submit for Review within the Stage sandbox.
    • After submitting for review, entities in the Stage sandbox are updated to the Queued for Review status. While in this status, you can use the Rescind button to remove your request for review. This allows you to update your configurations and resubmit for review as needed.
    • Once the review begins, entities are updated to the In Review status. You cannot rescind your submission or push changes to Stage while in this status.
  2. Once the Epic Games Store team completes its review, your submission is then either approved or identified as having issues found.
    • If your submission has been approved, you can proceed to the Launch Your Product phase.
    • If issues have been found in your submission, use the feedback from the Epic Games Store team to update your configurations in the Dev sandbox. Those changes must then be pushed to the Stage sandbox and resubmitted for review.

Launch Your Product

Overview

Once your product has been reviewed and approved by the Epic Games Store team, you are ready to launch your product! In this phase, you will push your product to Live sandbox to make it available for purchase in the Epic Games Store.

Estimated time to complete: 15 minutes

Prerequisites:

  • Complete the Onboarding phase.
  • Complete the Finalize and Test Configurations phase.
  • Complete the Submit for Final Review phase.
    • Note: Your submission must be approved in order to launch your product. If you received feedback during the Review phase that indicated one or more issues were found in your submission, you must complete the required updates and resubmit your product for review.

Process

  1. Once your submission has been approved, you can publish your product by pushing to the Live Sandbox. You can choose to either publish your product immediately or at a scheduled time.
    • Navigate to the Release Management section and ensure your entities in the Stage sandbox are in the Ready for Live status. Then, complete one of the following as appropriate:
      • Immediate publish: In the Stage sandbox, click Push to Live. On the next screen, click Push to Live.
      • Scheduled publish: In the Stage sandbox, click Push to Live. On the next screen, enable the Schedule Publish toggle and enter the desired date and time. Then, click Schedule Publish.
    • Notes:
      • The visibility of your page(s) depends on the dates you configured for your offers. For example, if an offer has dates set in the future, that offer is not available to users until that time.
      • If you push your product to the Live sandbox with a discovery date set to a future time, your product is not visible on the storefront until that time. However, once your product is in the Live sandbox, it is indexable on the public web. Ensure you are ready for your product to be public information before pushing it Live.
      • Scheduled pushes can be canceled prior to their specified date through the Cancel button available on the Release Management screen prior to their specified date.
  2. Congratulations - you just launched your product on the Epic Games Store! You can monitor your product’s performance post-launch through the Dev Portal. Navigate to Your Product > Analytics > Epic Games Store to access a variety of key insights and reports about your product.
