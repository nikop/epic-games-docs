Your Party

How to allow players to join or leave a party on the Social Overlay.

3 mins to read

Introduction

On the Social Overlay, a player with an Epic Games account can invite another player with an Epic Games account, who is playing the same game, to join a party. The players in a party can do most of the activities they can do in an EOS SDK lobby or session, including:

  • RTC functions: voice communications.
  • Invite a player.
  • Mute a player.
  • Kick a player.

The reason players may want to join a party is so that they can use voice communications to discuss a game before they start playing. For example, they might agree on which in-game customization each player is going to use.

For more information on:

  • How to create a lobby, see Lobby Interface.
  • How to create a session, see Session Interface.

Register Callback

When you initialize the EOS SDK, you need to register callback EOS_Lobby_AddNotifyLeaveLobbyRequested if you want to use a lobby, orEOS_Sessions_AddNotifyLeaveSessionRequested for a session, after you successfully call EOS_Platform_Create. This is so that the EOS service can notify you when players use the Social Overlay to leave a party.

For more information on how to:

  • Initialize the EOS SDK, see the Platform Interface documentation.
  • Use EOS_Lobby_AddNotifyLeaveLobbyRequested if you want to use a lobby, orEOS_Sessions_AddNotifyLeaveSessionRequestedfor a session, see Allow Players to Leave Your Party.

Allow Players to Join a Party

A player with an Epic Games account can invite another player with an Epic Games account, who is playing the same game, to join a party. If the invited player accepts the invitation, every player in the party sees the new player on the Social Overlay on the Friends list under the Your Party heading.

To do this, players need to:

  • Invite Another Player to a Party

    On the Social Overlay, a player with an Epic Games account goes to the profile of another player with an Epic Games account playing the same game and selects INVITE.

    Note: the EOS SDK automatically adds a player to a lobby by themselves when they first sign in to your game.

  • Select the Invitation to a Party

    The invited player receives a notification that a player invited them to play your game. They either select the invitation or go to their Friends list and select the invitation (they can see it in the Invitations to Play heading), or go directly to the profile of the player that invited them.

  • Accept or Ignore the Invitation

    On the Social Overlay, the invited player can accept or ignore the invitation. To do this they select ACCEPT INVITE or IGNORE invite.

Allow Players to Leave a Party

A player with an Epic Games account can leave a party on the Social Overlay. To do this they go to their Friends list and select LEAVE PARTY.

When a player leaves a party, the Social Overlay calls EOS_Lobby_AddNotifyLeaveLobbyRequested if you use a lobby, orEOS_Sessions_AddNotifyLeaveSessionRequestedfor a session, and sends a notification to you. EOS_Lobby_AddNotifyLeaveLobbyRequested includes a handle to the lobby and EOS_Sessions_AddNotifyLeaveSessionRequested includes the name of the session associated with the leave request. You need to do any necessary cleanup in your game and then call EOS_Lobby_LeaveLobby with the handle of the lobby so that you identify the correct lobby or session for the player to leave.

Show Players Across Multiple Platforms in a Party

On the Social Overlay, a player sees the list of other players in their party under the heading Your Party. They see the following:

  • Platform friends

    These are Epic friends that are playing your game on the same platform.

  • Friends not on the same platform

    These are Epic friends that are playing your game but are not on the same platform.

  • Players that are not friends

    These are players that are playing your game but are not Epic friends. These players are not listed separately, the player only sees a number that represents how many players are not friends. For example, +2.

  • Friends that they blocked

    These are friends that they block using the Social Overlay. The player sees the blocked player and the label “Blocked”.

Remove Callbacks

When you shut down your game, you need to remove the callback you registered earlier that handles notifications for players with Epic Games accounts who leave a party:

  • EOS_Lobby_AddNotifyLeaveLobbyRequested
  • EOS_Sessions_AddNotifyLeaveSessionRequested

To do this call the following:

  • EOS_Lobby_RemoveNotifyLeaveLobbyRequested
  • EOS_Sessions_RemoveNotifyLeaveSessionRequested
