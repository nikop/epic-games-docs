Manage Regions and Ratings

Upload product ratings and determine your regions for distribution.

14 mins to read

Regions and Ratings is one of two sets of configuration you must complete to publish your product on the Epic Games Store. See the following documentation for guidance: Configure Store Settings.

You manage your product's regions for distribution, as well as its age ratings through the Developer Portal in the Regions and Ratings section of the Store Settings page. You manage these settings in the following ways:

  • Obtain IARC (International Age Rating Coalition) ratings for your product.
  • Upload traditional ratings from other official rating authorities.
  • Determine which regions to distribute your product in.

The sections below describe these.

Page Navigation

To manage ratings and distribution, go to the Store Settings page.

To do this:

  1. Go to your product page:
    • In the Developer Portal (dev.epicgames.com/portal), from the left navigation, under Your Products, click the name of your product. (Note: You might have to click Back to organization before you can do this.)
  2. Once you are in your product page, from the left navigation, click Epic Games Store.
  3. From the left navigation, click Store Settings.
  4. On the Store Settings page, click Regions and Ratings.

Ratings

While the Epic Games Store does not require all products to be rated, ratings are required in the following circumstances:

  • Some regions, such as South Korea or Australia, require ratings from specific authorities in order to distribute products in those areas.
  • Products that have received ratings from any official rating authority must display those ratings on their product page in the storefront.
  • Products leveraging blockchain or NFT technology in any capacity must be rated in order to be distributed on the Epic Games Store.
You can choose to distribute your product on the Epic Games Store as an unrated title in regions without specific requirements. However, unrated products are treated as having the highest-level maturity rating available in regions where they are distributed.

To best reach potential users on the storefront, obtaining ratings for your product is highly recommended. If you do not have ratings for your product, you can easily obtain an IARC rating through the Epic Games Store publishing tools.

Before You Begin

Before configuring any ratings for your product, consider the following:

  • Australia, Brazil, and Russia require IARC or region-specific ratings for products to be distributed in these areas. To learn more, review IARC Ratings.
  • GRAC (Game Rating and Administration Committee) ratings are legally required to distribute products in South Korea. This requirement applies to any playable builds (including demo and early-access builds) that are available to the public. To learn more, review Distribution in Korea.
  • Ratings are configured at the base product level. Any ratings provided are used for your base product offer as well as any other offers associated with your product (such as editions or DLC). Configuring ratings per offer is not currently supported.

Completing the Initial Setup

When first configuring ratings for your product, you are presented with a short series of questions about IARC Ratings. These questions guide you in completing one of the following required actions:

  • Addding an existing IARC rating to your product by entering your IARC Certificate ID or Global Rating ID.
  • Obtaining an IARC rating for your product by completing the IARC questionnaire.
    • Note: When you start the questionnaire, the following restrictions apply:
      • You must complete the questionnaire. You cannot opt out of IARC ratings after you start the questionnaire.
      • You have three hours to complete the questionnaire from the time that you start it. If you take longer than three hours, then you must start the questionnaire again.
      • You cannot push your product to the Stage sandbox until you complete the process below and receive your rating.
  • Opting out of using IARC ratings for your product. (You can choose to opt in at a later time, as needed). After you opt out, you can choose to upload traditional ratings for your product or leave your product unrated.
    • Note: Unrated products are treated as having the highest-level maturity rating available in regions where they are distributed.

Detailed steps and additional information for each of these actions are provided in the sections below.

Tip: If you have already configured ratings for your product, review the appropriate section below for information on updating existing ratings:

  • Managing IARC Ratings
  • Managing Traditional Ratings

Add an Existing IARC Rating to your Product

If you have an existing IARC rating for your product, you must upload that rating in order to distribute your product on the Epic Games Store. To ensure a successful launch, we recommend completing this process before submitting your product for review.

To add an existing IARC rating to your product, follow the steps below:

  1. In the Developer Portal, go to the Regions and Ratings section of the Store Settings page. To do this, see Page Navigation at the top of this page.
  2. In the Regions and Ratings section, click Edit. Then, click Ratings.
  3. When asked if you already have an IARC Certificate ID or Global Rating ID (GRID) for your product, select Yes and click Next.
  4. Enter your IARC Certificate ID/Global Rating ID and contact email. Then, click Next.
  5. You are then presented with an overview of the regional ratings assigned to your product as per IARC guidelines. Review this information for accuracy, then click Confirm to upload the ratings to your product.

Once your IARC rating is uploaded, you can manage this information at any time by returning to the Regions and Ratings section of Store Settings. To learn more, review the Managing IARC Ratings section

Obtain an IARC Rating for your Product

If you do not currently have an IARC rating for your product, you can receive one by completing the IARC questionnaire. This step is recommended to promote an optimal user experience.

If you start the IARC questionnaire, you must complete it. You cannot opt out of IARC ratings after you start the questionnaire. You have three hours to complete the questionnaire from the time that you start it. If you take longer than three hours, then you must start the questionnaire again. Additionally, you cannot push your product to the Stage sandbox until you complete this process and receive your rating.

To obtain an IARC rating for your product, follow the steps below:

  1. In the Developer Portal, go to the Regions and Ratings section of the Store Settings page. To do this, see Page Navigation at the top of this page.
  2. In the Regions and Ratings section, click Edit. Then, click Ratings.
  3. You are first asked if you already have an IARC Certificate ID or Global Rating ID (GRID) for your product. Select No and click Next.
  4. When asked if you would like to obtain an IARC rating by completing the questionnaire, select Yes. Then, click Next.
  5. On the popup, enter your contact email. Then, click Yes, Continue to access the questionnaire on the IARC site.
  6. Fill out the questionnaire on the external site. The questionnaire asks about the content of your product (such as the presence of violence or profanity) to help determine the appropriate ratings.
  7. After completing the questionnaire, you are presented with an overview of the regional ratings assigned to your product. Click Finished to the bottom right to return to the Epic Games Developer Portal.
    • Tip: If you believe an assigned rating does not accurately represent your product, you can click Back to the bottom left to return to the questionnaire and update your answers as needed.

Upon returning to the Dev Portal, your IARC rating is automatically uploaded. On the Product Ratings page, you can review your IARC Certificate ID/Global Rating ID and the list of assigned regional ratings.

Once your IARC rating is uploaded, you can manage this information at any time by returning to the Regions and Ratings section of Store Settings. To learn more, review the Managing IARC Ratings section.

Opt Out of Using IARC Ratings

If you do not want to use IARC ratings, follow the steps below. You can choose to opt in at a later time.

  1. In the Developer Portal, go to the Regions and Ratings section of the Store Settings page. To do this, see Page Navigation at the top of this page.
  2. In the Regions and Ratings section, click Edit. Then, click Ratings.
  3. You are first asked if you already have an IARC Certificate ID or Global Rating ID (GRID) for your product. Select No and click Next.
  4. You are then asked if you would like to obtain an IARC rating by completing the questionnaire. Select No. Then, click Next.
  5. On the popup, confirm your decision to opt out of IARC ratings.

After opting out of IARC ratings, you can either upload traditional ratings or leave your product unrated. If you decide to leave your product unrated, it will be treated as having the highest-level maturity rating available in each region where your product is distributed.

For information on obtaining IARC ratings at a later time, review the Obtain an IARC Rating after Initial Opt-Out section.

Manage IARC Ratings

Once the initial setup is complete, you can manage IARC ratings for your product at any time in the Developer Portal's Store Settings page, in the Regions and Ratings section. (To go to the Regions and Ratings section, see Page Navigation at the top of this page.)

From here, you can:

  • Obtain an IARC rating for your product if you previously opted out.
  • Re-take the IARC questionnaire to accommodate changes or updates to your product.
  • Retrieve your IARC ID for ratings portability.

Obtain an IARC Rating after Initial Opt-Out

If you opted out of IARC ratings during the initial setup, you can opt in at any time directly through the Dev Portal.

The process described below prompts you to complete the IARC questionnaire. Note the following:

  • If your product is already live on the storefront, then you have 24 hours to complete the questionnaire after you start the process below. If you do not complete the questionnaire in 24 hours or less, then your product is suspended from being purchasable on the Epic Games Store.
  • When you start the questionnaire, the following restrictions apply:
    • You must complete the questionnaire. You cannot opt out of IARC ratings after you start the questionnaire.
    • You have three hours to complete the questionnaire from the time that you start it. If you take longer than three hours, then you must start the questionnaire again.
    • You cannot push your product to the Stage sandbox until you complete the process below and receive your rating.

To obtain an IARC rating for your product, follow the steps below:

  1. In the Developer Portal, go to the Regions and Ratings section of the Store Settings page. To do this, see Page Navigation at the top of this page.
  2. In the Regions and Ratings section, click Edit. Then, select Ratings.
  3. Click Get IARC Ratings. Then, select Add IARC Start IARC Questionnaire.
  4. Follow the onscreen instructions to access the questionnaire on the IARC site.
  5. Fill out the questionnaire on the external site.
    The questionnaire asks about the content of your product (such as the presence of violence or profanity) to help determine the appropriate ratings.
  6. After completing the questionnaire, you are presented with an overview of the regional ratings assigned to your product. Click Finished to the bottom right to return to the Epic Games Developer Portal.
    • Tip: If you believe an assigned rating does not accurately represent your product, you can click Back to the bottom left to return to the questionnaire and update your answers as needed.

Once you have completed this questionnaire, your IARC rating is automatically added to the Developer Portal. On the Product Ratings page, you can review your IARC Certificate ID (also known as the Global Rating ID) and the list of assigned regional ratings.

Re-Take IARC Questionnaire

Any time updates are made to your product that impact your original answers on the IARC questionnaire, you must retake the questionnaire to obtain an updated rating.

To retake the questionnaire, follow the steps below:

  1. In the Developer Portal, go to the Regions and Ratings section of the Store Settings page. To do this, see Page Navigation at the top of this page.
  2. In the Regions and Ratings section, click Edit. Then, select Ratings.
  3. Follow the onscreen instructions to access the questionnaire on the IARC site.
  4. Fill out the IARC questionnaire with answers that reflect your updated product.
  5. After completing the questionnaire, you are presented with updated ratings for your product. Click Finished to the bottom right to return to the Epic Games Developer Portal.
    • Tip: If you believe an assigned rating does not accurately represent your product, you can click Back to the bottom left to return to the questionnaire and update your answers as needed.

Once you have completed this questionnaire, your IARC rating is automatically updated in the Developer Portal, and the Product Ratings page reflects any changes.

Access IARC Certificate ID for Ratings Portability

If you already have an IARC rating for your product, you can reuse this rating when onboarding your product to other storefronts. To facilitate this, you need your IARC Certificate ID or Global Ratings ID.

To retrieve your ID, follow the steps below:

  1. In the Developer Portal, go to the Regions and Ratings section of the Store Settings page. To do this, see Page Navigation at the top of this page.
  2. In the Regions and Ratings section, click Edit. Then, select Ratings.
  3. Your ID displays at the top of the Product Ratings page. Click the Copy icon to quickly retrieve your ID.

Automatic IARC Rating Updates

When you obtain an IARC rating for your product, your product is assigned a set of regional ratings from a variety of rating authorities as per IARC guidelines. At times, you may notice ratings from a specific authority change automatically without any action from you.

Such updates can occur due to the following:

*The rating authority amended the rating determination made by the automated IARC system. *A traditional rating was provided for your product that supersedes the determination made by the automated IARC system.

If one of your assigned ratings has changed, you will receive an email notification directly from IARC. No action is required from you to facilitate these updates.

Managing Traditional Ratings

In the Regions and Ratings section of Store Settings, you can upload product ratings obtained from traditional rating authorities, such as ESRB or PEGI. If your product has received any traditional ratings, you must upload this information regardless of whether you choose to leverage IARC ratings.

If your product has a traditional rating pending, you cannot distribute your product on the Epic Games Store until you receive and upload that rating.

To upload traditional ratings, follow the steps below:

  1. In the Developer Portal, go to the Regions and Ratings section of the Store Settings page. To do this, see Page Navigation at the top of this page.
  2. In the Regions and Ratings section, click Edit. Then, select Ratings.
  3. Select the rating you wish to upload from the list of supported authorities.
    • Note: If needed, you can replace a specific rating you received through the IARC system with a traditional rating. To do so, select the authority from the list and choose Replace with Traditional Rating.
  4. Complete the brief form to provide details about your product’s rating. Depending on the authority selected, completing this form may include:
    • Uploading the rating license or certificate issued by the rating agency in PDF format.
    • Selecting the appropriate rating category.
    • Specifying the content descriptors and interactive elements that influence the rating category (i.e., violence, language, or in-game purchases).
  5. Review the information provided for the rating authority, then click Save Changes.
  6. Repeat steps 3-5 as needed for each traditional rating available for your product.

Ratings and the Review Process

In order to distribute your product on the storefront, you must submit it for review by the Epic Games Store team. As part of this review, the ratings, descriptors, and interactive elements you include in your uploaded ratings are compared against the certificates you provide.

You can add, remove, or edit ratings and their associated descriptors and interactive elements even if you have already pushed your product to your Stage sandbox. For example, you can add ratings for additional regions in which you intend to launch your product. To update your rating information, first complete the desired changes in the Dev sandbox. Then, push those changes to the Stage sandbox and submit them for review.

Content Descriptors and Interactive Elements

In addition to the official rating classification, some authorities also require descriptors, interactive element disclosures, or both. Descriptors and interactive elements indicate to users why your product received its specific rating. If your rating certificate includes any descriptors or interactive elements, you must provide this information along with your rating classification.

Regions

In addition to uploading rating information, you can also determine the regions in which you plan to distribute your product through the Store Settings section of the Dev Portal.

Certain regions (such as Australia, Brazil, Russia, and South Korea) require products to have IARC or other region-specific ratings. In order to select these regions through the steps below, you must first upload the appropriate ratings to your product. If ratings have not yet been added for a region with specific requirements, you will not be able to select those regions.

To determine your product's distribution regions, follow the steps below:

  1. In the Developer Portal, go to the Regions and Ratings section of the Store Settings page. To do this, see Page Navigation at the top of this page.
  2. In the Regions and Ratings section, click Edit. Then, click Regions.
  3. The list of Available Regions displays to the right. By default, all regions without rating requirements are selected. Update the selections in this list as appropriate for your product.
  4. Once all desired regions are selected, click Save Changes.
