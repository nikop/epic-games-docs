Epic-managed distribution on the Epic Games Store
Under 1 min to read

What is self-managed vs Epic-managed distribution?

To publish a product on Epic Games Store, you generally fall within one of these different categories:

  • Epic-Managed: Our team sets up your product manually. The pages in this section of the Epic Developer Resources Documentation apply to this distribution method.
  • Self-Managed: You use tools within the Developer Portal to set up your own product pages, achievements, pricing, offer , builds, and updates on the Epic Games Store with less dependence on our team. If you do not have Epic-managed distribution for any of your products, this section of the documentation does not apply to you. For assistance with these products, refer to the Epic Games Store section of the documentation.
  • A mixture: Your organization might contain both product types.

How do I know if I have Epic-managed distribution?

To quickly identify whether a product is self-managed or Epic-managed, take a look at the left-hand navigation menu in the Developer Portal. If your product is self-managed, you'll see Release Management, Store Settings, Offers, and more. If your product is Epic-managed, you'll see fewer options: Builds, Pages, and Discount Management Tool.

You can also review the distribution agreement at any time through the Agreements section of the Dev Portal.
