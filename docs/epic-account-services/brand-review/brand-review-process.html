Brand Review Process

Verify the brand associated with your game.

5 mins to read

Brand review enables your game (or software application) to integrate with EOS Epic Account Services. The brand review process verifies your game’s brand with Epic Games. Your brand is the company or real-world organization that is associated with your product.

Before starting the brand review process, see the documentation Brand Review Introduction.

Note on the terms “product” and “application”
In the Developer Portal during the brand review process, note the following:

  • “product” represents your game or software application. (For example, the game “Fall Guys”.)
  • “application” represents an implementation of your product for Epic Account Services. An application associated with your product has settings for brand, user permissions (consent), and linked clients. (For example, the settings for Fall Guys' brand, user permissions and linked clients in Epic Account Services is an application.)

This set of brand review pages uses “product” to mean game or software application and "application" to mean a product's Epic Account Services settings.

How to Submit (or Resubmit) Your Product for Brand Review

To start the brand review process, you must follow the steps below.

Step 1: Verify your brand's website domain.

  • See the Domain Verification documentation for guidance.

Step 2: Submit your product for brand review in the Developer Portal.

To do this:

  1. In the Developer Portal (dev.epicgames.com/portal), make sure you are in the correct organization relating to your product. To do this:
    a. Select Organization from the top of the left navigation’s dropdown menu (in the image below, the organization name is “Your…”).
    b. Follow the onscreen instructions to choose your organization.
    c. This displays a list of your organization’s products at the bottom of the left navigation menu under Your Products.

  1. Click your product name from under the list of Your Products in the left navigation menu (in the image below, the product name is “Your Product Name Here”).
    This displays the following on the left navigation:

    • A list of services your product might use (Epic Games Store, EOS Game Services, EOS Epic Account Services, and Analytics).

  2. In the left navigation menu, click Epic Account Services.
    This displays your product’s Epic Account Services page.

  3. To create a new application of your product, click Create Application.
    (Click Configure for an existing application of your product).
    This displays a pane showing the application settings tabs. It defaults to the Brand settings tab.

  4. In the Brand settings tab, follow the on-screen instructions.
    See the Brand Settings section of this page for details.

  5. After you have completed the required fields in the Brand settings tab, select Submit for review.

Step 3: Wait for Epic Games to contact you.

  • Epic Games evaluates your application’s brand.
    See the Brand Review Introduction page's section on Evaluation Criteria for details.

  • Epic Games notifies you of the review results by email.
    See the Brand Review Results section of this page for details.

Changes to your brand details during the brand review proces

After you submit your brand details for review, you cannot make any changes to your brand details during the review.

Note the following points:

  • If you want to change your brand details aftrer you have submitted them for review, you must cancel the brand review you have already started and start a new brand review.
  • If your product has already passed brand review and is in a live environment, you can change the brand settings. However, any changes you make to your product’s brand details do not affect your live environment until they have successfully passed the brand review.
  • You can change other product settings, such as permissions or OAuth client configuration at any time, independent of your product brand review status.

Brand Settings

Users see your application’s brand details on the consent dialog that Epic Games displays when users access your product.

You add your brand details in the Brand Settings page of the Developer Portal (shown below).

To access the page, see step 2 of the section in this page How to Submit or Resubmit Your Product for Brand Review.

On the left of the Brand Settings tab, you can see a preview of an Epic Games consent dialog. When your product uses Epic Account Services, this is the dialog that displays to your users. It requests their permission to share their information with your product through Epic Account Services.

On the right of the Brand Settings tab, there are configuration fields that you must complete.

Complete the fields to start the Epic Games brand review process.

The fields are as follows:

  • Application Name - A non-unique string that serves best to identify the Epic Account Services application of your product. By default it’s auto-populated with your product’s name as listed in the Developer Portal but you can change this.
  • Application Website - A verified website domain for your organization. See the Domain Verification documentation for guidance.
  • Privacy Policy URL - You must provide a link to your privacy policy.
  • UPLOAD APPLICATION LOGO - Your brand or product logo must be in PNG or JPEG format and 128x128 pixels.

Brand Review Results

When the review is complete, Epic Games notifies you of the results through email. You can use the Developer Portal to check the status of your review at any time.

Case Number

Your brand review submission is assigned a unique case number. It is in the Developer Portal on your product’s Epic Account Services page. You can access the page as follows:

  • dev.epicgames.com/portal menu: /your organization name/ > Products: /your product name/ > Epic Account Services.

Use this case number if you contact Epic Online Services support. See the Support page for details on how to get support.

Successful Review

If your brand is verified by Epic Games, your product’s brand settings are automatically applied to the live environment, and the audience restriction and warning messages are automatically removed.

Failed Review

If your brand is not verified by Epic Games, we will communicate why your brand failed and work with you to update your content and resubmit.

Note:

  • Failed reviews for products (or software applications) that are already in a live environment do not affect the live state of your product.
  • If your brand was previously verified, your players continue to see the brand content that is already verified.
