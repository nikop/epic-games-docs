Manage Your Organization

Set up your organization in the Epic Games Store publishing tools.

5 mins to read

An organization is a group (usually a company or other business, such as a game studio or publisher) that creates and owns products. When you use Epic Games Store, you create an organization during the onboarding process. You can manage your organization on the Organization page of the Developer Portal at any time.

In the Developer Portal, you associate your Epic Games account with an organization. Each Epic Games account that you associate with an organization is a “member” of the organization.

With the appropriate subscriptions, members of an organization can access these Epic Games tools and services:

Epic Tools

  • Unreal Engine
  • Twinmotion
  • MetaHuman
  • RealityCapture

This page and this set of documentation does not provide information on these tools. To access the relevant documentation, see the Epic Tools section of the Developer Portal - Introduction page.

For an overview of the Epic data hierarchy relating to Epic Games Store and EOS, see the following documentation: Developer Portal - Introduction - Your Organization and Products.

Organizations and the Epic Games Store

To access the Epic Games Store publishing tools, you must set your organization type to Enterprise. This allows the owner of your organization to accept the required agreements on behalf of your organization as a legal signatory. You can manage your organization type on the Settings tab of the Organization page.

Publishing Tool Permissions

To access the Epic Games Store publishing tools, members of your organization must have the following permissions:

  • Organization permissions: Product Management
  • Store permissions: Publishing Dashboard

You can assign permissions to custom roles to define the level of access you require for that role. The table below lists the Store permissions that you can assign to a role:

Permission Description
Pages Allows users to manage your product's storefront pages, such as the Product Home page, from the Product dashboard and the Pages tab.
Localization Allows users to manage text and asset localization for your store presence.
Offers Allows users to manage offers associated with your product, including the base game offer, from the Product dashboard and Offers tab.
Store Settings Allows users to configure your product's store settings, ratings, and regions, from the Product dashboard and Store Settings tab.
Release Management Allows users to manage sandboxes and push entities through the publishing workflow, from the Product dashboard and the Release Management tab.
Artifacts and Binaries Allows users to manage artifacts and binaries associated with your product, from the Product dashboard and the Artifacts and Binaries tab.
Publishing Dashboard Provides access to the main product dashboard, and is required to set up other permissions.
Offers Live Update Allows users to manage Offers dates live update settings for your product, from the Product dashboard.
Artifacts Live Update Allows users to manage Artifacts live update settings for your product, from the Product dashboard.
Access Keys Allows users to manage access key settings for your product, from the Product dashboard and Access Keys tab.
Custom Price Tiers Allows users to manage custom price tiers settings for your product, from the Product dashboard and Custom Price Tiers tab.
Marketing Assets Allows users to manage marketing assets settings for your product, from the Product dashboard and Marketing Assets tab.
Discounting Allows users to manage discounting settings for your product.

Submission Fee and Product Questionnaire

To access the Epic Games Store publishing tools for your product, you must complete the following tasks:

  • Pay Submission Fee: You must pay a submission fee of $100 USD (or local currency equivalent), plus local taxes if applicable, for each product you intend to distribute. While this fee is non-refundable, you recoup it in the payment made to you after your product has earned at least $1000.00 in revenue from Epic Games Store purchases (and in-game purchases using the Epic Payment Platform). If you have any questions about the submission fee, create a private discussion using this support website: eoshelp.epicgames.com.
  • Complete Product Questionnaire: You must complete a preliminary questionnaire about the product that you want to distribute on the Epic Games Store. Your responses to these questions are non-binding and are intended to help us identify the best support for your product.

You only complete these tasks once per product.

To complete the tasks, follow the steps below.

  1. Create a product.
    To do this: a. Go to the Developer Portal (dev.epicgames.com/portal). b. Click Create Product from the left navigation. (Notes: You might have to click Back to organization before you can do this. If you can't see Create Product, you do not have the appropriate organization member role to do this.) c. Follow the onscreen instructions to create a product.
  2. Click your product name from under the list of Your Products in the left navigation menu. This displays the following on the left navigation:
    • A list of services your product might use: Epic Games Store, EOS Game Services, EOS Epic Account Services, and Analytics.
    • The Product Settings option.
  3. Click Epic Games Store.
  4. Follow the onscreen insructions.
    • Tip: You can exit this process at any time and return later to complete it. Return to this by following steps 2 and 3 above.
  5. Once you have completed these steps, you can see the Epic Games Store section of the Developer Portal for your product.

Make sure that you also complete any required agreements for your organization and provide your trader, tax, and payout information. You must complete these tasks to distribute your product on the Epic Games Store.
