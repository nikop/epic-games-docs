Epic-managed distribution on the Epic Games Store
6 mins to read

This article covers functionality for products using Epic-managed distribution. If the options described are not available to you, you might be using self-managed distribution. For documentation applicable to your experience, refer to the Epic Games Store section in Developer Resources.

This page explains how to set up your product on the Epic Games Store if you are using Epic-managed distribution. Find answers on how to get started, how to publish, and how to update your products.

How to Work with the STORM Team

The Epic Games Store Operations and Release Management (STORM) team helps partners to release content on the Epic Games Store. The STORM team serves four main functions:

  • Enable you to onboard, prepare, launch, and manage your products on the Epic Games Store.
  • Deliver configuration support and guidance for offer and artifact setups and updates.
  • Guide the release process through an aligned launch plan and readiness confirmation.
  • Provide information and materials to make publishing a frictionless, efficient experience throughout each step of the release process.

A member of the STORM team, your Release Manager, is assigned to your organization. They will be your main point of contact with the team. When you have signed your deal, your product enters the onboarding process and your Release Manager contacts you to guide and support your efforts. Your Release Manager is responsible for the backend configuration of your product setup. After the configuration is completed, the team provides you with the following credentials so you can complete your setup:

  • Organization ID
  • Code name
  • Product ID
  • Sandbox ID
  • BuildPatch Tool Client ID and Client Secret
  • Staging artifact and live artifact

What to Expect

The process starts when you sign an agreement to publish a product on the Epic Games Store. Your Release Manager assists your team through the entire release process, from onboarding to launch and beyond. The timeline below shows the high-level steps you can expect. Note that the timeline is an example only, and your publishing journey might vary.

Step Timeline What to Expect
0 - Sign Deal --- Welcome to Epic Games Store
* Onboarding begins upon deal signature execution.
* Introduction to Release Manager via your Business Development (BD) contact.
1 - Get Provisioned At least 4 weeks prior to launch Kick-off introductions and initial review of game release plan and goals.
* Epic Games gives you access to the tools and gain access to resources that enable asset collection and dedicated communication channels.
* You complete the Title Questionnaire and Korea distribution survey.
2 - Learn the Requirements At least 3 weeks prior to launch Tool access and understanding distribution and content requirements.
* You get Developer Portal access.
* You must read our requirements and guidelines for content and distribution.
* You must read our requirements for multiplayer products. A key requirement is that you must provide a cross-play enabled build at least two weeks prior to any public launch to allow sufficient time for Store testing and verification.
3 - Deliver Builds and Assets --- You submit builds and assets for store presence and promotions. You work with your Release Manager to do the following:
* Partner Organization and Product setup
* Submit assets for the base product Product Home Page in the Developer Portal’s Asset Uploader Tool (other tools may be used for downloadable content and special editions)
* Catalog Offer setup
* Sales and marketing setup
* Price setup
* Cloud Saves setup
* Conduct checks on the build in the Dev Portal’s BuildPatch Tool
* Test the purchase flow
* Finalize your Product Home Page, addressing feedback from Release Management
4 - Prep for launch 1-2 weeks prior to launch Product validation and storefront page approval.
* Your final Product Home Page content is locked in.
* You confirm go/no-go with your Release Manager 48 hours before launch.
* You ensure the final build is uploaded to Production artifact.
* Your Release Management provides you with the URL of your Product on the Epic Games Store (can be provided earlier if needed).
5 - Store launch Day of launch Go live based on your release date and time.
We confirm launch execution.
* You confirm the product is live and working.
* Analytics become visible in Dev Portal 24 hours post launch.
6 - Post launch --- You keep us informed of any plans for updates, DLC, and so on. To help this happen as smoothly as possible, provide us with information as early as possible. See our Service Level Agreements (SLA) for reference.
You can request an opt-in email list for your product.
* You might choose to expand your catalog offers.
* You work with your Release Manager for any Product Home page updates.

Ecommerce Get Started Guide

The Epic Games Store offers an extensive set of ecommerce capabilities. You can use them to manage purchasable or redeemable items related to your product, such as downloadable content (DLC) or virtual currency. The system is set up as a hierarchy, so you can optimize bundles within your product offerings. The Ecommerce Get Started Guide explains the essential terminology and provides common use cases of how you can use the Ecommerce API to support in-game and back-end functionality.

FAQ

In Which Countries is the Epic Games Store available?

The Epic Games Store is available to players in all countries in the world, except where it is prohibited by U.S. law, such as in North Korea and Iran. Certain countries might have additional legal requirements that you may need to comply with in order to distribute your products there.
