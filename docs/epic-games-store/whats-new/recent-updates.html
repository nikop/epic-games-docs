Recent Updates

Recent releases and documentation updates for the Epic Games Store publishing tools.

Epic Games Store Only

2024

April 23, 2024 Notification

Developer Portal

We have made some updates to the user interface in the Developer Portal. We have made the following improvements:

  • Epic Tools
    • Added the Epic Tools section for your organization. Use this to purchase and assign seats for Unreal Engine, Twinmotion, RealityCapture, and MetaHuman.
  • Your Products
    • Updated the “Products” section on the left navigation to “Your Products”. This makes clear the distinction between your products and the tools provided by Epic Games (known as “Epic Tools”) that you can use.
  • Organization Profile
    • Added a Profile tab in the Organization page. Use this to configure and update your organization’s profile, and choose to make your organization public on the Epic Developer Community website.
  • Roles
    • Added an organization member default role. An account holder with the organization member default role has no permissions on the Developer Portal. Use this when you want to assign an Epic Tool seat to your team member, but do not need to give them further permissions in your organization.
  • VAT Information
    • Added a Claim VAT/Sales Tax section. Use this to specify your company’s VAT details. We add your VAT number to purchases you make on the Developer Portal.

Note: These updates do not change the service of Epic Games Store or Epic Online Services.

Further information:

  • Documentation: Developer Portal
  • Changelog: Go to the Developer Portal at dev.epicgames.com/portal and click View changelog.

February 15, 2024 Release

Promotional Content
We added a new requirement that if your offer promotes your own (or someone else's) brand, product, or business, then you must declare it as promotional content in the offer settings. If you make this declaration, then the Epic Games Store labels the offer as promotional content.

This applies only If your organization is new to the Epic Games Store. If your organization is already on the Epic Games Store, then you have until February 17, 2025 before you need to make this declaration on your offers.

Further information:

  • Documentation: Manage Offers

Trader Information
Before you can sell products on the Epic Games Store, you must put your Epic Games Store organization through trader verification.

This applies only if your organization is new to the Epic Games Store. If your organization is already on the Epic Games Store, then it has until February 17, 2025 to go through trader verification.

Further information:

  • Documentation: Manage Trader, Tax, and Payout Details—Complete Trader Verification

February 9, 2024 Release

Specify Deploments on Artifacts
If your product uses the Epic Online Services (EOS) SDK, then your build must specify the correct deployment when it initializes the EOS SDK. To make sure that your build can identify the correct deployment, you can now set a deployment ID on your artifact. When the Epic Games Launcher starts your build, it sends this deployment ID to your build as a command-line parameter.

  • Documentation: Manage Artifacts: Select Deployment ID

October 16, 2023 Release

Epic First Run
Epic First Run is an opt-in program for a new product’s first six months. The program boosts a developer’s revenue share on the Epic Games Store from 88% to 100%. After the six-month run, developers continue to benefit from the standard 88% revenue share.

You can opt your product into the program if it has never been available elsewhere and if it is only available on the Epic Games Store during the program’s six months. Note that not all products are eligible. See the documentation for details.

Further information:

  • Epic First Run announcement: store.epicgames.com
  • Documentation: Epic First Run

Now On Epic
Now On Epic is an opt-in program for a product’s first six months on the Epic Games Store if the product is already available elsewhere. The program boosts a developer’s revenue share on the Epic Games Store from 88 to 100. After the six-month run, developers continue to benefit from the standard 88 revenue share. Note that not all products are eligible. See the documentation for details.

Further information:

  • Documentation: Now on Epic

Some products on the Epic Games Store are designed and rated for mature audiences. However, all main storefront content must be suitable for general audiences: this is a rating of PEGI 12, ESRB Everyone 10+, or lower.

If your product contains in-app purchases, this information must be clearly stated on your store presence. This helps foster a transparent and trustworthy store experience for store users.

To provide users with awareness of in-app purchases, you must create an FAQ page for your product that includes a specific set of questions.

Epic Games is committed to facilitating fair and competitive pricing for your products but ultimately, the prices you set are up to you. August 22, 2023 Release

Developer Portal: Bulk Discounts
You can now create discounts in bulk by filling out and uploading a template provided by Epic Games. With this template, you can create multiple discounts across multiple products with a single file upload.

Note: You cannot create custom and preset campaign discounts in a single upload. You must upload separate templates for these.

To create discounts in bulk:

  1. Go to the Developer Portal (dev.epicgames.com/portal).
  2. Select <your product name>.
  3. Select Epic Games Store > Discounting.
  4. Click Upload Bulk Discount.

May 25, 2023 Release

Improved Offer Management
For improved offer management, you now unlink base offers from Edition-type offers in the Dev sandbox. This improvement helps ensure updates that you make to base offers are reflected in their associated editions.

Previously, when you made an update to base offer, you had to push both the base offer and any associated editions to your Stage sandbox. If you did not push the editions to Stage, the base offer updates were not reflected in those editions.

With this update, you no longer need to push editions to Stage along with your base offer. You can now unlink your base offer from associated editions prior to making updates. When the base offer is updated, relinking that offer to your editions ensures they are associated with the most up-to-date version of your base offer.

Further information:

  • Documentation: Manage Offers

April 13, 2023 Release

The following updates have been made to the Epic Games Store publishing tools:

  • A new Legal role (note: as of October 2023, this is called the Signatory role) is now available in the Dev Portal for Enterprise organizations. This role grants users Legal Management permissions, which allows them to complete agreements on behalf of their organization. Previously, agreements could only be completed by organization owners. March 28, 2023 Release

New! Dedicated pages for Edition-type offers are now available, providing a similar experience to pages for other offer types, such as demos. Pages comprise your product's presence on the storefront and display content about your base product and other offers.

Previously, Edition offers were only listed along with your base product on your Product Home page. Now, when a user on your Product Home page selects an edition, they are taken to a page containing text and media tailored to that edition.

Further information:

  • Documentation: Manage Pages

Note:

  • Pages are automatically generated for Edition offers created after the release of this update. However, Edition pages are not retroactively generated for offers created prior to March 28, 2023.
  • For existing Edition offers, the following options are available:
    • Create new pages: In the Pages section of the publishing tools, you can manually create new pages for each Edition offer you have available.
      • Documentation: Manage Pages
    • No action: If no action is taken, your Edition offers will retain their current behavior and continue to display on your store presence as prior to this update. This update does not remove or alter any edition information from your product on the storefront.

January 24, 2023 Release

New! IARC ratings are now supported for products distributed on the Epic Games Store!

The International Age Rating Coalition (IARC) provides a globally streamlined age classification process for digital products. This system helps to ensure users are given consistent access to established and trusted ratings across platforms.

With this release, partners can now easily obtain IARC ratings through the Epic Games Store publishing tools and display that information on product pages in the storefront.

June 23, 2022 Release

New! When setting dates for product offers, users are now presented with a guided walkthrough for a simplified, more intuitive experience. The new experience prompts users to answer a series of questions related to their offer to ensure relevant details are captured up front (i.e., Was this offer previously released on PC or Mac?). Additionally, the new experience features an offer date timeline visualization that helps boost understanding and overall accuracy of configurations.

Note: While the guided experience presents information in a new format, the specific details requested have not changed with this update.

  • The guided experience displays by default when configuring offer dates for a new offer. Similarly, the guided experience displays when configuring dates for the first time on an existing offer.
  • When updating previously-configured offer dates, users can select Guided Experience to walk through the steps and complete their desired changes. Alternatively, users can leverage the classic offer date view to complete their desired updates from a single page.

March 9, 2022 Release

Localization Template Updates- FAQ and About Section Contents

  • Before, section contents for sections containing multiple text blocks and images (About and FAQ sections) were combined into a single cell in the localization template, including markdown code for images. Now, content blocks are separated into separate rows in the localization file, and no longer contain information about images.
