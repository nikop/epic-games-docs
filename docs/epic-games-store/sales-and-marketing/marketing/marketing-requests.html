Marketing Overview

Learn more about marketing your product on the Epic Games Store.

Epic Games Store Only
7 mins to read

If you are using self-service Epic Games Store publishing tools, any changes or updates related to your Product Setup or Product Home pages should be made through the Developer Portal. If you need assistance, search or post in the Epic Developer Community forums.

For partners using an alternate method of distribution, please email your Epic Games Store account team for help.

This page introduces the various marketing channels and the associated formats and guidelines of the related marketing assets you can use across these channels.

When you use the Epic Games Store logo in your marketing materials and communications channels, use our Logo Guidelines.

Remember—marketing starts with you. Epic Games Store channels are designed to amplify the content that our players want. When you build demand for your product, this allows automated areas of the Epic Games Store to drive further discoverability of your content.

Epic Games Storefront Merchandising

The following marketing assets are displayed on the Epic Games Store main page, and subsequent landing pages in the storefront such as our Sales & Specials and Free Games destinations. They can be used to feature selected products, games, promotional sales, and updates.

Note that the specifications (formats/sizes/guidelines) of marketing assets differ from the assets on your product home page. For information about the required assets on your product home page, see Manage Pages.

Epic Games Store Social Channels

Introduction and Terminology

The Epic Games Store has four official Epic Games Store social channels that have aggregated millions of followers:

  • Facebook
  • X (formerly Twitter)
  • Instagram
  • TikTok

These accounts are the Epic Games Store's official main channels for its English-speaking audience. The Epic Games Store team provides updates on games regularly and excites its audiences about new game announcements, upcoming releases, and more!

Your product might be promoted there for various occasions in one of two ways:

  • Bespoke native posts: Text paired with a video, GIF, link or static asset, posted from the above Epic Games Store official channels.
  • Social Shares: Amplification on Twitter with retweets, and Instagram with shares to our IG Stories. We do not share posts on Facebook.

Social Tone

  • Our voice is friendly, engaging, and informative, with some playfulness thrown in.
  • The channel character for the voice is based on 'your friend who just wants to talk to you about cool video games all day in a relatable way.' Memes and emojis are encouraged.
  • Posts always include some reference to the content of the game being discussed but always in our own tone and voice.
  • Content we typically promote: Gaming updates, game trailers, publisher sales and any new content a partner would like to promote.
  • Content we generally do not promote:
    • Realistic war content (depending on the global climate), excessive violence or gore, or anything overtly sexual or inappropriate in nature.
  • Note: We will adapt social copy as needed to fit the tone above. We will however endeavor to keep all core messaging present.

How to Talk About the Epic Games Store in Your Social Media Post

When you mention the Epic Games Store at the beginning of a sentence, write one of the following:

  • "The Epic Games Store"
  • "The #EpicGamesStore"

When you mention the Epic Games Store in the middle of a sentence, write one of the following:

  • "....the Epic Games Store"
  • "....the #EpicGamesStore"

Epic Games Store YouTube Channel

The Epic Games Store YouTube Channel offers another platform to amplify your videos and engage with the Epic Games Store community. The Epic Games Store actively promotes a wide range of content including reveal and launch trailers, tutorials, and gameplay videos.

The Epic Games Store also features videos in its themed YouTube playlists (such as the Summer Game Fest playlist and the Game Awards playlist).

Guidelines and Specifications for YouTube Videos

  • Video
    • MP4 preferred, H.264 compression, 60 FPS or less
    • Stereo/mono AAC (LC) audio recommended
    • Aspect Ratio: 16:9
    • Max File Size 1GB

Video endslates (end cards or end screens)
