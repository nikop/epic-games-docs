Organizations

Overview of the organization management tools in the Developer Portal.

7 mins to read

In Epic Games Store and Epic Online Services (EOS), data relating to your account in the Developer Portal is organized in a hierarchy, with “organization” at the top.

What is an Organization?

An organization is a group (usually a company or other business, such as a game studio or publisher) that creates and owns products. In the Developer Portal, you associate your Epic Games account with an organization. Each Epic Games account associated with an organization is a “member” of the organization.

Members of an organization manage products that you add to the Developer Portal on behalf of the organization. Your organization can have multiple members.

For an overview of the Epic data hierarchy, see the following documentation:

  • Developer Portal - Introduction - Your Organization and Products

How to Set Up Your Organization

When You First Sign In
When you first sign-in, the Developer Portal prompts you to create a new organization associated with your account.

After You Have Signed In
You can add a new organization at any time. To do this once you are in the Developer Portal (dev.epicgames.com/portal):

  1. From the left-navigation, click the name of your organization at the top (shown as “Your…” in the image below).
  2. In the Choose your organization pop-up window, click Create organization and follow the on-screen instructions.

Join an Existing Organization
To associate an Epic Games account with an existing organization, you can invite new members to join. For further information, see the Members section of this page.

If you are a member of more than one organization, at sign-in a prompt asks which organization you want to access. After you have signed in, you can select a different organization at any time.

Organization Page

The Developer Portal has an Organization page, where you can manage your organization members, their roles, and view agreements.

Page Navigation

  • To access the Organization page:
    1. Go to the Developer Portal (dev.epicgames.com/portal).
    2. Click Organization from the left navigation. (Note: You might have to click Back to organization before you can do this.)
  • There are multiple tabs on the Organization page, Click the tab’s title to see the contents of the tab. Note: You might not see all the tabs. Some tabs are only visible to organization admins or owners.

Organization Page Tabs

Members

Each member has a role within the organization. The role gives them permission to access different tools in the Developer Portal.

Add New Members
You must have the correct permissions (as an admin or owner) to add a new member to your organization. If you have the correct permissions, click Invite new. (If you do not have the correct permissions, you can’t see this button.)

The Developer Portal adds the new member to your organization once they have accepted the invitation.

Remove Members or Change Access Rights
Once a member is part of your organization, an owner or admin can remove or change the access rights of a member. To do this:

  1. Click the checkbox against the member name on the member list.
  2. Choose the action you want from the pop-up menu at the bottom of the page.

Notifications

This page is only visible to an organization’s admins or owners. Here, you can set up email notifications regarding updates to Epic Games Store and Epic Online Services, as well as notifications regarding your products.

Note: You can only add email addresses for domains that are already verified. (To verify a domain, see the Domains section of this page.)

Roles

Each member has a role. A role grants a member permission to access different tools in the Developer Portal.

The default roles are listed on the Roles tab of the Organization page in the Developer Portal. Admins can create custom roles.

Custom Roles
Admins can create custom roles for their organization. You can allocate your organization members bespoke permissions to access Epic Games Store and EOS features in the Developer Portal.

Tax Info

If you are distributing a game on the Epic Games Store, using the publishing tools, you must complete the fields in this tab to set up your tax profile and a hyperwallet account.

Further information:
You can find out about this in the Fortnite Creator Help Center articles and Fortnite Creator documentation. The process for Fortnite creators and developers distributing products on the Epic Games Store is the same.

  • Fortnite Creator Help Center article at www.epicgames.com/help:
    • Set up Your Account for Tax Profile and Hyperwallet
  • Fortnite Creative documentation at dev.epicgames.com/~/fortnite-creative:
    • Set up Your Tax Profile
    • Set up Your Hyperwallet

Payout Info

This tab shows money paid to your organization. You can only see the contents of this tab if you have set up your tax profile and Hyperwallet account. Use the Tax Info tab to do this.

Agreements

Use the Agreements tab to review the active agreements with Epic Games for your organization.

Profile

This is the representation of your organization in the Developer Portal and, if you choose it, in the Epic Developer Community (EDC) (dev.epicgames.com/community).

Your organization profile includes a profile image (for example a company logo), a cover image (a background image), text to introduce your organization, and social media links (for example a link to your organization’s LinkedIn profile).

The default profile setting is private: only members of your organization can see your profile in the Developer Portal. If you make your profile public then all users of the EDC can also see it.

Your Organization’s Profile as a Public Profile
You can choose to make your organization profile public on the EDC (dev.epicgames.com/community).

If you do this, members of your organization are associated with your organization publicly on the EDC. This means that any activity by members of your organization on the EDC is publicly associated with your organization’s profile.

For example, when a member of your organization creates guides (known as “tutorials”) in the EDC or answers questions in the EDC forums, all users of the EDC can see this activity on the profile of your organization, as well as the member’s personal profile. This helps users of the EDC discover your organization and any tutorials your organization's members create.

Make your Organization’s Profile Public
To do this:

  1. In the Developer Portal (dev.epicgames.com/portal), click Organization from the left navigation.
  2. On the Organization page, click Profile.
  3. You must add the following to your organization’s profile content, if you don’t you can’t make it public:
    1. Enter text for the organization biography.
    2. Upload a profile image
    3. Upload a background image.
  4. Select Make this profile public.

Settings

In the Setting tab, you can configure your organization type, domains, and console developer access.

General Settings

Use this section to configure information about your organization such as changing its name, country, or adding an address or support email.

Claim VAT/Tax

Use this section to add information about your organization’s tax (or VAT) status. If your organization is registered to pay tax (or VAT), Epic Games might not have to charge you tax (or VAT) on any products or services it provides to your organization.

Organization Type

An admin or owner can configure your organization type. Your organization type can either be an individual or a company:

  • Individual: The default type for an organization. This type indicates that all of the members of the organization are acting on their personal behalf.
  • Enterprise: This type is available for incorporated entities. It indicates that your organization represents a legal entity.

Note: If you change your organization type to Enterprise, you cannot change it back to Individual.

Domains

Use this tab to add and verify website and email addresses (“domains”) associated with your organization.

Verifying your domain protects you, all your players or users, and Epic Games from malicious activities, such as brand spoofing, phishing, malware, stolen identity, and copyright infringement.

For guidance on the domain verification process, see the documentation: EOS Epic Account Services - Domain Verification.

You must add and verify your domain in order to complete the following processes:

  • Email notifications.
    (Documentation: See the Notifications section of this page.)
  • Brand review.
    This is essential to using Epic Account Services in your product.
    (Documentation: EOS Epic Account Services: Brand Review.)

Console Developer Access

You can only access the EOS SDK for console downloads, and their associated documentation, if you have the appropriate permissions. This section provides information on how to get the permissions.

Configure Console Developer Access
To find out how to get access to the EOS SDK for console downloads, and their associated documentation, follow the steps below.

  1. In the Developer Portal (dev.epicgames.com/portal), click Organization from the left navigation.
  2. On the Organization page, click Settings.
  3. In the Settings tab, scroll down to Console Developer Access.
  4. Locate the console you want to develop games, and click Configure. This displays the console’s access-request page.
  5. Follow the on-screen instructions.
