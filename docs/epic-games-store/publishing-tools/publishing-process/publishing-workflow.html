Publishing Tools Workflow

Get started distributing products on the Epic Games Store.

27 mins to read

Epic Games Store publishing tools provide you with everything you need to distribute your product on the Epic Games Store. With these tools, you can maintain your product’s store presence, create offers, manage price tiers, and more.

To ensure a successful launch of your product on the storefront, follow the publishing tools workflow described in this page. This workflow consists of the following steps:

  • Step 1. Onboarding
  • Step 2. Publish Your Product Page (Early Awareness)
  • Step 3. Finalize and Test Configurations
  • Step 4. Submit for Final Review
    This step has details of the Epic Games Store Publishing Service Level Agreement.
  • Step 5. Launch Your Product

Each step includes an estimated time to complete. Note that these are general guidelines only and are subject to change. Times can vary based on unique business needs, volume of requests, or the level of configuration required.

Before You Begin

You are going to use the Epic Games Developer Portal to set up and manage your product (game or other software application). The Developer Portal is a browser-based tool you use to set up and configure your product on the Epic Games Store, but you can also use it for the following purposes:

  • Set up Epic Online Services (EOS), both EOS Game Services and EOS Epic Account Services, for your product.
    Documentation: Epic Online Services (EOS) Overview.
  • Set up user seats for Epic Tools (Unreal Engine, Twinmotion, MetaHuman, or RealityCapture).
    Documentation: Developer Portal Introduction - information on the Developer Portal and links to Epic Tools documentation.

Note: You do not need to set up EOS or Epic Tools to follow the Epic Games Store get started steps.

About Onboarding

The onboarding step described below takes you through the following sub-steps:

  • Create an Epic Games account and organization on the Developer Portal.
  • Set up your organization to access Epic Games Store distribution.
  • Add your game or software application as a product in the Developer Portal for Epic Games Store distribution.

You might have already completed some of the onboarding sub-steps before the publishing tools launched in March 2023. If you set up an organization but have only implemented Epic Online Services (EOS - Game Services or Epic Account Services) or not yet implemented any services, you can migrate your product to using Epic Games Store.

To do this:

  1. Sign in to the Developer Portal (dev.epicgames.com/portal).
  2. On the left navigation, under Your Products, click the name of the product you want to migrate to using Epic Games Store.
  3. Click Epic Games Store.
  4. Follow the onscreen instructions. Note that you must migrate products one by one.

Help from Developer Support
If you need any help migrating, contact Epic Games Developer Support.

To do this:

  • Create a private discussion on eoshelp.epicgames.com.
  • Set the topic to Epic Games Store and include the following in your request:
    • Your organization ID
      • To find this:
        1. Sign in to the Developer Portal (dev.epicgames.com/portal).
        2. On the left navigation, click Organization.
        3. On the Organization page, click Settings.
        4. Look for ORGANIZATION ID.
    • Your product ID(s)
      • To find this:
        1. Sign in to the Developer Portal (dev.epicgames.com/portal).
        2. On the left navigation, under Your Products, click the name of the product.
        3. On the left navigation, click Product Settings.
        4. The Product Settings page defaults to the SDK Download and Credentials tab. Scroll down this tab to the PRODUCT section. Look for PRODUCT ID.

Step 1. Onboarding

Onboarding involves two parts:

  • A. You create your account, organization and product in the Developer Portal.
  • B. You complete the admin process.

Estimated time to complete: Part A takes 5 minutes. Part B can take up to 10 business days, depending on your location.

Prerequisites: None

Before you begin
Prepare the following:

  • High-level details about your product (game or other software application), including its name and a short description.
  • Payment information for the submission fee.
  • A named person who can accept the Epic Games Store legal agreements. (You will add them as a member of your organization with the appropriate permissions.)
  • Contact information for your organization.
  • Tax information for your organization, such as W9/W8-BEN information.
  • Payout account details for your organization. This is the account where Epic Games routes payments.

Process

A. Create Your Account, Organization, and Product

  1. Go to the Developer Portal (dev.epicgames.com/portal) and follow the onscreen instructions to create an Epic Games account and an organization.
    (If you already have an Epic Games account, you can sign in to your existing account and continue; you don't have to create a new account.)
  2. Optional: Add team members to your organization.
    When you set up the organization, you become the owner of it. You can add additional team members in the Organization page.
    To do this:
    1. On the left navigation, click Organization. The Organization page defaults to the Members tab.
    2. Follow the onscreen instructions to invite new members and set their roles.
      For further guidance, see the documentation: Manage Your Organization - Organizations and the Epic Games Store.
  3. Create your product.
    To do this:
    • On the left navigation, under Your products, click Create Product and follow the onscreen instructions.

B. Complete the Admin Process

  1. On the left navigation, select the product you just created, listed under Your products.
  2. On the left navigation, click Epic Games Store to access the Epic Games Store pages. Before you can access them, you must complete the following tasks:
    • Agreements
      Accept the following Epic Games Store agreements:
      • Required: Epic Games Store Distribution agreement.
      • Recommended: Epic Games Store - Email Sharing Rider.
        Documentation: Email Sharing Rider.
      • Recommended: Epic Games Store Achievements.
        Documentation: Epic Games Store Achievements.
    • Fee
      Pay the required per-product $100 USD submission fee.
      If you have questions about the submission fee, create a private discussion with Epic Games Developer Support at: eoshelp.epicgames.com.
    • Questionnaire
      Complete the preliminary product questionnaire.
      This questionnaire collects essential information about your product to help the Epic Games Store team understand how to best support you throughout the distribution process. Responses to the questionnaire are non-binding.
  3. Complete your trader verification, create your tax profile, and activate your payout account.
    • This process can take up to two weeks to complete.
    • Documentation: Manage Trader, Tax, and Payout Details.
  4. Before you continue, make sure you review the following documentation for information on the Epic Games policies for distributing on the Epic Games Store: Requirements and Guidelines.

Step 2. Optional: Publish Your Product Page (Early Awareness)

Once you have completed onboarding, you can choose to publish your product page to the storefront for early awareness. If you do not wish to create early awareness at this stage, you can skip this step and proceed to Step 3. Finalize and Test Configurations.

  • If you have not yet launched your product on other storefronts, we recommend that you complete this step to spread early awareness of your product.
    We recommend that you publish your product page when you announce your product. This ensures users know it will be available on the Epic Games Store. It also allows users to wishlist your product and receive notifications (if they have opted-in).
  • If you have already launched your product on other storefronts, you might choose to skip this step and proceed to Step 3. Finalize and Test Configurations.

To publish your product page for early discovery, you must provide preliminary configurations for elements of your product's Epic Games Store presence. These elements, known as "entities", are as follows:

  • Store Settings (Product Configuration, Regions and Ratings)
  • Base Offer and Offer Dates
  • Home Page
  • Optional: Localization

Note: You do not need to fully configure these entities in this step. In Step 3. Finalize and Test Configurations, you can provide additional information and upload your product binary files (executable code file and any supporting files) to prepare for launch.

Estimated time to complete: 1 week

Prerequisites:

  • Complete Step 1. Onboarding.
  • For guidance on the sandbox environments and which entities you can configure, see documentation: Release Management.

Before you begin: To complete this step, prepare the following:

  • Metadata (for example, genre and features), distribution regions, and pricing information for your product.
  • Storefront content for your product page: text and media (for example, images, videos).
  • Localized storefront content. (Epic Games offers a free localization service for some text, details are below.)
    • Recommended: Localized text
    • Optional: Localized media

A. Store Settings: Product Configuration, Regions and Ratings

Documentation:

  • Configure Store Settings

To configure store settings, go to the Store Settings page. To do this:

  1. On the left navigation, click Store Settings.
  2. On the Store Settings page, locate the Product configuration tile and click Edit.
  3. Follow the onscreen instructions to add essential details about your product.
  4. Close the Product Configuration page. On the Store Settings page, locate the Regions and ratings tile and click Edit.
  5. Follow the onscreen instructions to obtain an IARC rating (or upload traditional ratings) and select the regions in which you plan to distribute your product.
    Epic Games Store does not require an IARC rating for your product, but we recommend it has one because it provides the best reach for potential users.

B. Base Product Offer

Documentation:

  • Manage Offers

Your base product offer is the product you plan to sell without any add-ons. Add-ons could be editions, season passes, chapters, or whatever you might add to your product. To configure your base product offer, go to the Offers page. To do this:

  1. On the left navigation, click Offers.
    The Offers page defaults to the Sandbox: Dev tab. Your product is listed in the Dev tab with an auto-generated base offer. Your product is assigned the default Type, Game.
  2. Click on your product's name.
  3. Follow the onscreen instructions to add details to the Required Information section of your base offer. For guidance on how to add dates, see Step 4. Submit for Final Review below.

A checkmark next to a section tile indicates that you have added details.

C. Offer Dates

Documentation:

  • Manage Offers: Dates

Continuing from B. Base Product Offer, above, in the Offer Details page of the Developer Portal:

  • Select the Dates tile and complete the Offer Dates Quick Start questions, using the guidance below.
  • Question 1 (Was this offer previously released on PC or Mac?)
    Follow the onscreen instructions.
  • Question 2 (When do you want this offer to be discovered?)
    Determines when your product page is visible to users on the storefront. This date does not determine when it becomes available for purchase.
    You can set a specific availability date or choose to have your product page published as soon as your base product offer is pushed to the Live sandbox.
  • Question 3 (When is this offer officially launching, or anticipating to launch, on the Epic Games Store?)
    Determines when your product is officially launched on the storefront.
    If you do not have a product build ready, set the launch date type to Estimated or Launch date unknown.
  • Guide to launch date types:
    • Specific: You must add a release-ready binary to the artifact for this offer.
    • Unknown: The launch date on your product page displays as "Coming Soon".
    • Estimated: You can provide a generalized date to display instead (For example, May 2030).
    • Estimated or Unknown: You must return during Step 3. Finalize and Test Configurations and provide a specific launch date to prepare for release.

    • Optional:

F. Submit for Review

After you have configured these settings, you can submit your product for review by the Epic Games Store team.

To submit your product for review, go to the Release Management page. To do this:

  1. On the left navigation, click Release Management.
  2. On the Release Management page, in the Dev sandbox tab, click Push To Stage.
  3. On the Push to Stage page, check the entities you have just configured (Product data: Product configuration, Product home, Regions and ratings and Offer and Page data: The name of your product). The Epic Games Store requires this to publish your product page.
    Note: Do not check entities under Artifact data.
    (If you have any additional offers or pages that are not ready to be published, leave those items unchecked.)
  4. Click Push to Stage. This action may take a few minutes to complete.
  5. After the Push to Stage is complete, click Submit for Review in the Stage sandbox.
    The Epic Games Store team now reviews your submission to ensure it meets content guidelines and other requirements. The review might take 2 or more days to complete.

G. Publish

Note: When you publish your product page, it becomes indexable on the public web, even if your discovery date is set to a future time. Ensure you are ready for your product to be public before completing this step.

After the Epic Games Store team has approved your submission, you can publish your product through the Release Management page. To do this:

  1. On the left navigation, click Release Management.
  2. On the Release Management page, in the Stage sandbox tab, click Push To Live. The availability of your product page in the storefront depends on how you configured your discovery date in Step 2 C. Offer Dates, above.

Step 3. Finalize and Test Configurations

Next, complete all of the configurations required to launch your product. This includes the following tasks:

  • Create offers.
  • Upload your product's binary files (executable code file and any supporting files) to the Epic Games Store.
  • Test to ensure your product and store presence are ready for launch.
  • Submit your product for review by the Epic Games Store team.

Estimated time to complete: Varies per product depending on specific configurations needed.

Prerequisites:

  • Complete Step 1. Onboarding.
  • Review the release management documentation to find out about the sandbox environments where you configure entities relating to your product. Documentation: Release Management.

Before you begin: To complete this step, prepare the following information:

  • A list of all offers (for example, editions or season passes) that you plan to apply to your product.
  • For all offers, prepare the following information:
    • Metadata (for example, genre and features), distribution regions, and pricing information.
    • Storefront media and text.
    • Localized storefront content. (Epic Games offers a free localization service for some text, details are below.)
      • Recommended: Localized text
      • Optional: Localized media
  • FAQ page content, if applicable.
    • Tip: FAQs are optional for most products, however you must provide them in certain situations. For example, FAQs are required for early access products, products using blockchain or NFT technology, or products featuring in-app purchases.
      For more information, see documentation: Epic Games Store distribution requirements.
  • Your product's binary files. (A file or set of files known as "a binary" or "binaries" interchangeably.)
    • Your product's binary files are the executable code and any supporting files that users need to run your product. For example, any of the following types of file can be a binary file: executable file, metadata file, configuration file.
    • In the Developer Portal and Epic Games Store, the terms "a binary" or "binaries" are interchangeable, regardless of how many binary files your product requires.
    • Note: Ensure your product's binary files meet the technical distribution requirements for the Epic Games Store and adheres to content guidelines.
      For information on this, see documentation: Requirements and Guidelines.

F. Binaries and Artifacts

Documentation:

  • Manage Artifacts

You must upload your product's binaries and attach them to an artifact.

What are binaries and artifacts?

  • Binaries
    Your product's binary files are the executable code file and any supporting files that users need to run your product.
    In the Developer Portal and Epic Games Store, binary files are known as "a binary" or "binaries". The terms "a binary" and "binaries" are interchangeable, regardless of how many binary files your product requires.
    The following are examples of binary files:
    • Types of file examples: executable file, metadata file, configuration file.
    • Types of content examples: offers, editions, season passes, chapters, or any other content you want users to download.
  • Artifact
    An artifact represents a single downloadable piece of content in your product, such as a game client or mod editor. You can attach multiple binary files to an artifact.

You must upload binaries and attach them to artifacts for your base product offer in order to complete Step 4. Submit for Final Review.

There are three sub-steps:

  • Check binaries
  • Upload binaries and attach to an artifact
  • Test binaries
Check Binaries

    Note: Some entities may have dependencies that prevent you from pushing them to Stage. You cannot push these entities until all dependencies are in the Ready for Stage status. During this step, ensure you select all dependencies for the entities included in your product launch.

Step 4. Submit for Final Review

When you have completed Step 3, you can submit your product for review by the Epic Games Store team.

During this step, the Epic Games Store team reviews the content you have added to your product's Epic Games Store presence, and your product binaries to ensure everything meets the Epic Games Store content guidelines and requirements for distribution.

Estimated time to complete: Varies depending on volume and product complexity.

Prerequisites:

  • Complete Step 1. Onboarding phase.
  • Complete Step 3. Finalize and Test Configurations phase.

Before you begin:

  • Make sure your product is ready for launch. To do this, see the following documentation:
  • Distribution requirements: Store Requirements Overview
  • Content Guidelines

Process

To submit your product for review, go to the Release Management page. To do this:

A. Submit for Review Through the Developer Portal

To do this:

  1. Sign in to the Developer Portal (dev.epicgames.com/portal), and make sure you are in the correct organization for your product.
    (To check, see Organization at the top of the left navigation in the Developer Portal. To change organization, click Organization and follow the onscreen instructions.)
  2. On the left navigation, under Your products, select the name of your product.
  3. On the left navigation, click Epic Games Store and then Release Management.
  4. On the Release Management page, in the Stage sandbox tab, click Submit for Review.

The Epic Games Store team now reviews your submission to ensure it meets content guidelines and other requirements. The review might take 2 or more days to complete.

B. Wait for the Review to Complete

  • After submitting for review, all entities in the Stage sandbox have the status Queued for Review.
  • While in this status, you can use the Rescind button to remove your request for review. Use this option to update your configurations and resubmit your product offer for review as needed.
  • When the Epic Games Store team begins the review, all entities in the Stage sandbox have the status In Review status. You cannot rescind your submission or push changes to Stage while there are entities in this status.

C. Receive the Review Results

  • After the Epic Games Store team completes the review, they give your submission one of two statuses: "approved" or "issues found".
  • If your submission is approved, you can proceed to Step 5. Launch Your Product.
  • If your submission has issues found, use the feedback from the Epic Games Store team to update your configurations in the Dev sandbox. Then push the updated configuration to the Stage sandbox and resubmit it for review.

Service Level Agreement
The service level agreement (SLA) is the minimum amount of time that it takes Epic Games support to reply to your request.

The SLA below is the default you have when you sign up to use Epic Games publishing tools.

SLA for General Support and Submissions

SITUATION SLA
General Support 1 - 3 business days
Build Testing (See related documentation.) 2 - 3 business days
Epic Games Store Achievements (See related documentation, specifically the how to test achievements section.) 2 - 3 business days
Multiplayer Titles - Crossplay is required if your product is distributed on other PC platforms (see related crossplay documentation). Note: You must submit your product binaries (your build) for final review 4 weeks before you launch to allow Epic Games to test them. 3 - 5 business days
Epic Account Services Brand Review (See related documentation.) 2 - 3 business days
Final Review Before Launch (See related documentation.) 3 - 5 business days

Step 5. Launch Your Product

After your product is approved by the Epic Games Store team, you are ready to launch your product on the Epic Games Store. You do this by pushing your product to the Live sandbox on the Developer Portal, we call this "publishing" your product. Publishing your product makes it available for purchase in the Epic Games Store. When you push your product to the Live sandbox, you can choose to either publish your product immediately or at a scheduled time.

Estimated time to complete: 15 minutes

Prerequisites:

  • Complete Step 1. Onboarding.
  • Complete Step 3. Finalize and Test Configurations.
  • Complete Step 4. Submit for Final Review.

Note: In order to launch your product on the Epic Games Store, the Epic Games Store team must have approved your product submission. If you received feedback that there are "issues found" in your submission, you must complete the required updates and resubmit your product for final review.

Process

After your product is approved by the Epic Games Store team, launch your product by publishing it on the Epic Games Store using the Release Management page. To do this:

  1. Sign in to the Developer Portal (dev.epicgames.com/portal), and make sure you are in the correct organization for your product.
    (To check, see Organization at the top of the left navigation in the Developer Portal. To change organization, click Organization and follow the onscreen instructions.)
  2. On the left navigation, under Your products, select the name of your product.
  3. On the left navigation, click Epic Games Store and then Release Management.
  4. Ensure your product's entities in the Stage sandbox are in the Ready for Live status.
  5. Choose an immediate publish or scheduled publish.
    • For immediate publish:
      1. In the Stage sandbox, click Push to Live.
      2. On the next screen, click Push to Live.
    • For scheduled publish:
      1. In the Stage sandbox, click Push to Live.
      2. On the next screen, enable the Schedule Publish toggle and enter the date and time you want to publish.
      3. Click Schedule Publish.

Congratulations - you have launched your product on the Epic Games Store!

Notes:

  • The visibility of your product's page or pages depend on the dates you configured for your offers. For example, if an offer has dates set in the future, that offer is not available to users until that time.
  • If you push your product to the Live sandbox with a publish date set to a future time, your product is not visible on the storefront until that time. However, once your product is in the Live sandbox, it is indexable on the public web. Ensure you are ready for your product to be public information before pushing it to Live.
  • You can cancel a scheduled publish before the scheduled publish date. To do this, click the Cancel button on the Release Management page.
